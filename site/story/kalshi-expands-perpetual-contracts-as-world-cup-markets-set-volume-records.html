Kalshi Expands Perpetual Contracts as World Cup Markets Set Volume Records

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 19:05 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi has launched an expanded perpetuals product line while World Cup event contracts have driven prediction-market trading volumes to new highs, according to TradingView.

Kalshi's expansion into perpetual contracts marks a structural broadening of its product suite beyond traditional binary event markets, according to TradingView coverage. Perpetuals allow traders to hold positions without a fixed expiration date, a format borrowed from crypto derivatives markets and now being adapted to regulated event-contract trading for the first time at meaningful scale.

Simultaneously, World Cup-related prediction markets have pushed trading volumes to record levels, signaling that major sporting calendars remain the single largest demand driver for retail participation in event contracts. The confluence of a marquee global sporting event with a product-line expansion suggests Kalshi is deliberately timing infrastructure rollouts to coincide with peak liquidity windows.

The CFTC-regulated exchange has been steadily widening its addressable market since winning its legal battle to offer election contracts in 2024. Adding perpetuals alongside high-traffic sports events represents a two-axis growth strategy: deepening instrument complexity for sophisticated traders while broadening the consumer funnel through familiar, high-interest tournaments.

Why this matters

Perpetual contracts without expiry dates introduce continuous funding-rate mechanics that prediction-market traders must price differently than standard binary outcomes, creating new arbitrage and hedging opportunities. Record World Cup volumes validate that sports remain the most reliable on-ramp for retail liquidity, giving Kalshi leverage in any future regulatory conversations about expanding permissible contract types. Operators and competitors like Polymarket should watch whether CFTC staff issue guidance on perpetual event contracts, as that ruling would reshape the entire industry's product roadmap.
