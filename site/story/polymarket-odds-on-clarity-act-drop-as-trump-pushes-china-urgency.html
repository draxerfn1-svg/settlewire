Polymarket odds on CLARITY Act drop as Trump pushes China urgency

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 16:43 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket contract odds on passage of the CLARITY Act fell sharply even as President Trump publicly pressured Congress with warnings about China competition, signaling trader skepticism about near-term legislative success.

Despite a vocal push from the Trump administration framing the CLARITY Act as a critical response to China's technological ambitions, Polymarket traders moved decisively against near-term passage, according to Yahoo Finance. The odds decline suggests the market views White House rhetoric as insufficient to overcome congressional obstacles, regardless of the geopolitical framing attached to the bill.

The CLARITY Act, which would establish a regulatory framework for digital assets including event contracts, has drawn attention from prediction-market operators like Kalshi and Polymarket because its passage could reshape how such platforms are governed in the United States. A clearer federal framework has been a long-standing demand from the industry.

Polymarket's crowd-sourced odds function as a real-time referendum on legislative probability, and the drop here reflects accumulated trader judgment that committee timelines, Senate dynamics, or competing priorities make passage within the contract's window unlikely — regardless of presidential urgency signals.

Why this matters

Falling CLARITY Act odds matter directly to prediction-market operators whose regulatory status hinges on the bill's fate — a prolonged limbo keeps Kalshi and Polymarket in a contested legal environment. Traders arbitraging political contracts should watch whether Trump's China framing shifts Senate vote counts, since any credible momentum could reprice these odds quickly. Regulation watchers should note that market-implied probability is now a live data point that lobbyists and lawmakers themselves may cite in legislative strategy.
