Italy re-blocks Polymarket, prediction market back on banned list

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 12:23 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Italian regulators have added Polymarket to the country's blocked-site list for a second time, cutting off access to the U.S.-based prediction market platform for users in Italy.

Italy's gambling regulator has placed Polymarket on its national blocklist once again, according to SBC News, marking at least the second occasion the decentralized prediction market has faced a formal access ban in the country. The move effectively prevents Italian internet users from reaching the platform without the use of a VPN or similar circumvention tools.

Polymarket, which is based in the United States and operates event-contract markets on topics ranging from elections to economic indicators, has faced repeated regulatory friction in multiple jurisdictions. Italy's regulator has broad authority to block unlicensed gambling or wagering-adjacent services, and prediction markets occupy an ambiguous legal zone in most European countries.

The repeated nature of the ban — described by SBC News with the headline use of 'once again' — suggests Polymarket had previously been removed from the blocklist, either through a technical lapse, an appeal, or a regulatory reassessment, only to be reinstated. The cycle underscores the persistent compliance challenge facing unregulated event-contract platforms seeking global user bases.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, Italy's renewed block signals that European regulators remain willing to act repeatedly against platforms that lack local licensing, even after initial bans are lifted. Operators watching Polymarket's expansion strategy should note that jurisdictional whack-a-mole enforcement can erode user growth in key markets without a formal legal resolution. Regulation watchers should track whether Italy's action influences peer EU member states to coordinate blocklist policies under existing gambling harmonization frameworks.
