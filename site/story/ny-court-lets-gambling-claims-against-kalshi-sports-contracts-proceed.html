NY Court Lets Gambling Claims Against Kalshi Sports Contracts Proceed

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 09:22 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A New York court declined to dismiss gambling-related claims against Kalshi's sports event contracts, allowing the legal challenge to move forward in what could be a landmark ruling for prediction-market operators.

A New York court upheld claims that Kalshi's sports event contracts constitute illegal gambling, according to Tokenist, refusing to throw out the case at an early stage. The ruling keeps alive a legal theory that could force Kalshi — the first federally regulated prediction-market exchange to offer sports contracts — to defend the legality of its core products in court.

Kalshi won CFTC approval to list sports-outcome contracts in 2024, a milestone that gave the company a regulatory green light at the federal level. The New York case tests whether state gambling laws can override that federal clearance, a tension that regulators and legal scholars have flagged as a critical unresolved question since the CFTC first began entertaining event-contract applications.

The outcome hinges on whether federal preemption under the Commodity Exchange Act shields Kalshi from state gambling statutes. No ruling on the merits has been issued yet; the court's decision merely allows the claims to survive dismissal, meaning discovery and further litigation lie ahead before any definitive judgment.

Why this matters

If New York courts ultimately rule that state gambling laws apply to federally approved event contracts, every prediction-market platform operating in the U.S. faces potential patchwork liability across 50 jurisdictions. Traders on platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi should watch this case as a leading indicator of whether sports-outcome markets can scale nationally. A ruling against Kalshi could also chill new CFTC event-contract applications and prompt Congress to clarify federal preemption.
