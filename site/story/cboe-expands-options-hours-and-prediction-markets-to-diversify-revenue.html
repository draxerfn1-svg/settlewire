Cboe Expands Options Hours and Prediction Markets to Diversify Revenue

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 07:12 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Cboe Global Markets is broadening its business into prediction markets and extended options trading hours, according to a Yahoo Finance analysis examining whether these moves redefine the exchange operator's diversification strategy.

Cboe Global Markets, the Chicago-based exchange operator behind the VIX volatility index, is actively pushing into prediction markets as part of a wider diversification effort, according to Yahoo Finance. The move positions Cboe alongside dedicated prediction-market platforms such as Kalshi and ForecastEx, raising the competitive stakes in a sector that has drawn intensifying regulatory and investor attention.

Yahoo Finance framed the expansion as a potential redefinition of Cboe's growth story, pairing prediction-market entry with plans to extend options trading hours — two structural changes that could meaningfully shift the firm's revenue mix. Cboe already operates ForecastEx, its CFTC-regulated event-contracts subsidiary, giving it a regulated beachhead in the space.

The prediction-markets segment has attracted mainstream financial players at an accelerating pace following the CFTC's evolving posture on event contracts. Cboe's institutional infrastructure and existing regulatory relationships give ForecastEx advantages over crypto-native competitors, though retail mindshare remains dominated by Polymarket and Kalshi heading into 2025.

Why this matters

Cboe's institutional heft entering prediction markets at scale could accelerate CFTC pressure to standardize event-contract rules, creating both opportunity and compliance risk for smaller platforms. Traders tracking Kalshi or ForecastEx market volumes should watch whether Cboe's extended-hours push draws liquidity away from crypto-native venues. A well-capitalized incumbent normalizing event contracts on traditional exchanges raises the floor for the entire sector's legitimacy.
