Polymarket World Cup betting volume reaches $3.9B, attracting Trump and Zuckerberg

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 19:58 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket's prediction-market wagering on the FIFA World Cup surpassed $3.9 billion in volume, drawing interest from Donald Trump and Mark Zuckerberg in the platform's explosive growth.

According to 24/7 Wall St., Polymarket's World Cup betting pool hit $3.9 billion, marking a landmark figure for the crypto-based prediction market platform and signaling mainstream breakthrough for event-contract markets. The involvement of high-profile figures like Trump and Zuckerberg — whether as participants, investors, or public endorsers — adds significant political and commercial weight to the platform's trajectory.

Polymarket has steadily grown from a niche decentralized forecasting tool into a major venue for geopolitical and sports event contracts, with the World Cup representing one of its largest single-event pools to date. The $3.9 billion figure dwarfs previous sports-related contract volumes on the platform and underscores growing retail appetite for liquid, publicly verifiable prediction markets.

The attention from figures like Trump and Zuckerberg could accelerate regulatory scrutiny as well as institutional legitimacy. Polymarket, which previously settled with the CFTC in 2022 over unregistered binary-options offerings, operates primarily via non-U.S. users, yet high-profile American interest inevitably draws Washington's gaze toward the platform's legal standing.

Why this matters

A $3.9 billion single-event volume benchmark gives regulators and Congress a concrete number to cite when debating event-contract oversight, making near-term CFTC rulemaking more likely. Trump's political orbit intersecting with Polymarket could cut both ways — protection from heavy-handed regulation or pressure to bring the platform onshore under formal licensing. For traders, liquidity at this scale means tighter spreads and greater price discovery reliability, but also heightened counterparty and regulatory-risk premiums to price in.
