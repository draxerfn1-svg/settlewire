Kalshi signs multiyear marketing deal with Pro Padel League

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 12:19 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Prediction markets exchange Kalshi has secured a multiyear marketing partnership with the Pro Padel League, marking the platform's latest push into mainstream sports sponsorship.

Kalshi has entered a multiyear marketing agreement with the Pro Padel League, according to Sports Business Journal. The deal gives Kalshi brand visibility across one of the fastest-growing racket sports in North America, extending the exchange's sponsorship footprint beyond digital channels into live sports environments.

The partnership follows Kalshi's successful legal fight to offer event contracts on U.S. soil and its subsequent aggressive expansion into sports-related markets. Padel, a racket sport blending tennis and squash elements, has attracted significant investment and audience growth in recent years, making it an attractive vehicle for brands targeting younger, affluent demographics.

For Kalshi, the Pro Padel League deal represents a deliberate effort to build consumer brand recognition alongside its regulatory and product wins. Prediction market platforms have increasingly pursued sports sponsorships as a mechanism to normalize event-contract trading among mainstream audiences, a strategy also seen among competitors like Polymarket as the sector matures.

Why this matters

Sports sponsorships signal that Kalshi is allocating meaningful marketing capital to consumer acquisition, a strategic shift traders should read as a precursor to higher retail volume and potentially new sports-event contracts tied to padel. For regulation watchers, high-profile brand deals increase political and regulatory visibility for the prediction-market sector, cutting both ways — attracting users but also legislative scrutiny. Competitors without equivalent sports-marketing budgets may find themselves disadvantaged in the race to become the default brand consumers associate with event-contract trading.
