Prediction Markets Reach $24B Monthly Volume as States Push Back

Published JUL 01, 2026 · 12:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Monthly trading volume on prediction markets has surged to $24 billion, triggering a wave of state-level regulatory resistance across the U.S.

Prediction markets have exploded in scale, with monthly volume hitting $24 billion according to Forbes, a figure that reflects dramatic mainstream adoption following high-profile political event contracts during the 2024 election cycle. The growth has transformed platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket from niche curiosities into financial infrastructure drawing serious institutional and retail attention.

The rapid expansion has not gone unnoticed by state regulators. According to Forbes, multiple states are actively pushing back against the spread of these platforms, raising concerns that event contracts on elections, sports, and other outcomes may overlap with gambling laws that states — not federal agencies — have historically controlled.

The tension sits at the heart of a federalism dispute: the CFTC has signaled that federally designated contract markets like Kalshi operate under its jurisdiction, effectively pre-empting state gambling statutes. States disagree, and some are pursuing their own enforcement actions, setting up a legal conflict that could reshape how and where Americans access these markets.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, state-level crackdowns could restrict access or force geo-blocking in key U.S. markets, fragmenting liquidity. Operators face compliance costs and legal uncertainty that may chill product expansion. Regulation watchers should monitor whether federal pre-emption arguments hold in court, since a ruling favoring states would fundamentally redraw the competitive landscape.
