Kalshi Launches Advanced Trading Terminal for Event Contracts

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 15:26 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi introduced a new advanced trading terminal in 2025, giving event-contract traders a more sophisticated interface with enhanced tools for executing and managing positions on its prediction markets platform.

Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction markets exchange, rolled out an advanced trading terminal designed to meet the demands of more active and professional traders. The new interface represents a significant upgrade from the platform's standard UI, according to Kalshi News, offering a more feature-rich environment for participants trading event contracts across politics, economics, and other categories.

The terminal is aimed at traders who require faster execution, deeper market data, and more granular control over their positions. While specific technical specifications were not fully detailed in the coverage, the launch signals Kalshi's continued push to attract sophisticated market participants beyond retail users, a demographic increasingly important as event-contract trading volumes grow.

The move follows Kalshi's landmark legal victory against the CFTC that cleared the way for it to operate election markets in the United States. With regulatory standing now established, the exchange has accelerated product development, and a professional-grade terminal is a logical next step toward competing with traditional financial infrastructure.

Why this matters

A professional trading terminal lowers the barrier for institutional and algorithmic traders to enter prediction markets, which could meaningfully deepen liquidity and tighten spreads on Kalshi's contracts. Thicker order books would make Kalshi more attractive as a price-discovery venue, putting pressure on rivals like Polymarket to match the tooling. Regulators watching event-contract markets may also take note, as more sophisticated participants increase the profile of the asset class.
