CFTC Opens Public Comment Period on Prediction Markets Rules

Published MAR 17, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The CFTC is soliciting public feedback on how to regulate prediction markets, signaling a formal regulatory review of the fast-growing sector.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission has launched a public input process focused specifically on prediction markets, according to WilmerHale's coverage of the development. The agency is seeking comments from industry participants, academics, and the general public on how event contracts and prediction markets should be governed under existing or new regulatory frameworks.

The move represents one of the most direct regulatory engagements the CFTC has undertaken with the prediction markets industry, which has expanded rapidly through platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket. Key questions likely under review include contract design standards, participant eligibility, and whether current designated contract market rules adequately address these novel instruments.

The public comment process is a standard but consequential step in U.S. rulemaking. Responses submitted during such periods can directly shape final rules, and major operators have a concrete opportunity to influence how the CFTC defines the boundaries of permissible event contracts — a question that has generated significant litigation and lobbying activity in recent years.

Why this matters

A formal CFTC comment process is a high-stakes moment for prediction market operators: the resulting guidance or rulemaking could either legitimize current product offerings or impose new restrictions. Traders should watch for signals on contract-type eligibility, as unfavorable rules could delist popular markets. Operators and legal teams should prioritize submitting comments to shape outcomes directly.
