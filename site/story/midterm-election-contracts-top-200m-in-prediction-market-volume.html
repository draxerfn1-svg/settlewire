Midterm Election Contracts Top $200M in Prediction Market Volume

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 16:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Traders on prediction markets have collectively wagered nearly $200 million on 2026 U.S. midterm election outcomes, according to NBC News.

NBC News reports that cumulative trading volume tied to the 2026 midterm elections has approached $200 million across prediction market platforms, a figure that underscores the rapid mainstreaming of event contracts as a political forecasting tool. The milestone comes well ahead of Election Day, suggesting volume could dwarf previous electoral cycles.

For context, the 2024 U.S. presidential election set records on platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi, with combined volume exceeding $3 billion. Midterm elections historically draw lower engagement, making a $200 million figure at this early stage a meaningful signal that retail and institutional participation in political contracts is accelerating between presidential cycles.

Platforms such as Kalshi, which won a landmark legal battle against the CFTC in 2024 to list political event contracts, and Polymarket, operating primarily on blockchain infrastructure, are the leading venues for this activity. Increased midterm volume strengthens the commercial case both companies have made to regulators and investors about the sustained demand for election markets.

Why this matters

A $200 million midterm volume figure arriving this early in the cycle suggests political event contracts have achieved durable retail traction, not just presidential-election novelty — a key variable for traders pricing platform-growth contracts. For regulation watchers, sustained high volume raises the probability that the CFTC will revisit or formalize its oversight framework for political markets before November 2026. Operators can expect this data point to be cited in lobbying efforts and potential congressional hearings on event-contract legality.
