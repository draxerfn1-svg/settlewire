Ninth Circuit grills Kalshi on sports contracts vs. betting in tribal lands appeal

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 10:53 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A Ninth Circuit panel pressed Kalshi lawyers in oral arguments to explain why its sports event contracts differ legally from sports betting under the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act in a tribal lands appeal.

The Ninth Circuit panel challenged Kalshi during oral arguments over whether its sports event contracts constitute illegal sports betting on tribal lands under the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act (IGRA). Judges questioned the company's attorneys on where the legal line falls between a CFTC-regulated event contract and a wager, according to Gambling Insider and Gaming America. The case centers on whether IGRA's tribal exclusivity provisions can block Kalshi from operating in California.

Kalshi has argued its products are federally regulated futures contracts overseen by the CFTC, not gambling subject to state or tribal jurisdiction. The tribal appellants contend that accepting money on the outcome of a sporting event is functionally identical to sports betting regardless of the regulatory label applied. The Ninth Circuit's questions, according to Focus Gaming News, suggested at least some skepticism toward Kalshi's jurisdictional framing.

The outcome carries broad implications: a ruling against Kalshi could effectively bar event-contract platforms from operating in states where tribal compacts hold exclusive sports-wagering rights. Kalshi won a landmark CFTC case in 2024 allowing its sports contracts to launch, but tribal IGRA claims represent a separate and potentially overlapping legal front that federal courts have not yet resolved.

Why this matters

A ruling that IGRA tribal exclusivity applies to event contracts would create a patchwork of state-level legal exposure for Kalshi and any competitor offering sports-outcome markets in the U.S. Prediction-market traders should watch closely: contracts tied to sports events could face sudden liquidity disruption if courts uphold tribal jurisdiction. Regulatory arbitrage between the CFTC's federal umbrella and state-level IGRA protections is now the central legal fault line for the entire event-contracts industry.
