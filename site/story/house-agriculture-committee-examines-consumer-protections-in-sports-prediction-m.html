House Agriculture Committee examines consumer protections in sports prediction markets

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 13:46 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The House Committee on Agriculture convened a formal hearing to scrutinize customer safeguards and market integrity standards specifically within sports event prediction markets, marking Congress's most direct legislative focus yet on the sector.

The House Committee on Agriculture, which holds jurisdiction over the Commodity Futures Trading Commission, held a dedicated hearing titled 'Examining Customer Protections and Market Integrity in Sports Event Prediction Markets.' The session signals growing congressional attention toward platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket that have expanded into sports event contracts following regulatory and legal battles with the CFTC.

The hearing arrives as the prediction markets industry has seen rapid growth in sports-linked event contracts. Kalshi won a landmark federal court ruling in 2024 allowing it to list election contracts, and subsequently moved aggressively into sports markets. Competitors including Polymarket and ForecastEx have similarly expanded their event-contract offerings, drawing scrutiny from both regulators and now lawmakers over how customer funds and market data are handled.

The Agriculture Committee oversees the CFTC, the primary federal regulator for designated contract markets that list event contracts. A congressional hearing of this nature can precede formal legislation, oversight letters to the CFTC, or requests for rulemaking — each of which could materially reshape compliance obligations for operators currently listing sports-related event contracts.

Why this matters

A formal House committee hearing on sports prediction market integrity is the clearest signal yet that Congress may legislate or pressure the CFTC to tighten rules, creating regulatory-uncertainty risk that traders should price into platform-specific positions. Operators currently listing sports event contracts face potential new customer-protection mandates — margin requirements, disclosure rules, or outright contract restrictions — that could compress liquidity. Prediction-market traders should watch for committee testimony transcripts and any draft legislative language as leading indicators of the regulatory direction.
