Kalshi sues Illinois and Ohio to block sports-market regulation

Published JUN 26, 2026 · 20:34 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi has filed lawsuits against both Illinois and Ohio, challenging state attempts to regulate or penalize its sports-event prediction contracts.

Kalshi is fighting on two state fronts simultaneously. According to Capitol News Illinois, the federally regulated prediction market has sued Illinois over the state's effort to classify its sports-event contracts as sports betting subject to state oversight. Kalshi argues its products are CFTC-regulated event contracts, not wagers under state gambling law.

On a separate front, DeFi Rate reports that Kalshi is also suing Ohio after the state levied a $5 million fine against the platform, again tied to its sports prediction market offerings. The Ohio action represents one of the largest state-level financial penalties yet directed at a federally licensed prediction market operator.

The dual lawsuits mark a significant escalation in the jurisdictional conflict between state gambling regulators and CFTC-authorized exchanges. Kalshi has consistently maintained that its federal designation preempts state gaming authority, a legal theory now being tested simultaneously in two separate court systems.

Why this matters

These cases could set binding precedent on whether CFTC designation shields prediction markets from state gambling enforcement — a question every operator in the space is watching. A Kalshi victory would clear the path for nationwide sports contracts without state-by-state licensing; a loss could fragment the market and raise compliance costs dramatically. Traders pricing political or sports contracts on any platform should watch these rulings as a leading indicator of regulatory risk.
