Polymarket Trader Bleeds $11M on World Cup 2026 Contracts

Published JUL 06, 2026 · 05:35 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A single Polymarket trader lost more than $11 million wagering on 2026 FIFA World Cup outcome markets over just ten days.

According to Yahoo Finance, one trader on Polymarket suffered losses exceeding $11 million across a span of roughly ten days placing bets on 2026 FIFA World Cup event contracts. The scale of the loss stands out even on a platform known for hosting large-position traders, making it one of the most dramatic single-account drawdowns in the platform's public history.

Polymarket's on-chain structure means all positions and losses are publicly visible on the blockchain, allowing analysts to trace the full scope of the damage. The World Cup markets in question likely involved national team outright winner or advancement contracts, which carry significant volatility given the tournament's unpredictable group-stage dynamics and the event itself still being more than a year away.

The episode underscores how prediction markets on sports and tournament outcomes can attract extremely large speculative positions, particularly as Polymarket has grown into a platform capable of sustaining eight-figure liquidity. It also raises questions about market design, position limits, and whether outsized single-trader exposure distorts pricing signals for other participants.

Why this matters

A loss of this magnitude on a single platform draws attention to Polymarket's risk management practices — or absence thereof — which regulators watching event-contract markets will note. For traders, it signals that World Cup markets may have been mispriced or illiquid enough to allow one actor to move odds significantly, creating potential arbitrage windows. Operators may face renewed pressure to implement exposure caps as the CFTC scrutinizes sports event contracts.
