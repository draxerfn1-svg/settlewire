State-by-State Legal Map: Where Sports Prediction Markets Stand

Published JUL 05, 2026 · 19:42 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A new CBS Sports breakdown examines the patchwork of state-level legality governing sports prediction market platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket across all 50 U.S. states.

According to CBS Sports, the legal status of sports prediction markets varies dramatically depending on where a user lives, with some states permitting access to federally regulated platforms like Kalshi while others have moved to restrict or scrutinize them. The analysis arrives as the sector faces growing public attention following Kalshi's court victory over the CFTC that opened the door to sports event contracts.

CBS Sports notes that Kalshi operates under CFTC oversight as a designated contract market, which provides a federal regulatory foundation — but that federal clearance does not automatically override state gambling or wagering statutes. Polymarket, which remains offshore and accessible to U.S. users only via workarounds, occupies a murkier position in the state-law analysis.

The report reflects a broader industry reality: prediction market operators are navigating a dual-layer regulatory environment where federal commodity law and state gaming codes can conflict. Several states with robust sports-betting frameworks have begun questioning whether event contracts constitute unlicensed gambling, setting up potential enforcement flashpoints.

Why this matters

For traders, state residency is now a meaningful risk variable — platforms may geo-restrict users preemptively to avoid regulatory exposure, shrinking liquidity pools. For operators, the CBS Sports map signals mainstream scrutiny that could accelerate state-level legislative action. Regulation watchers should track whether any state attorney general moves against a CFTC-licensed platform, which would force a direct federal-versus-state legal confrontation.
