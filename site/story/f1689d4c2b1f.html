Meta Weighed Kalshi Acquisition Before Building Own Prediction App

Published JUN 30, 2026 · 20:33 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Meta explored purchasing prediction market exchange Kalshi before ultimately deciding to develop its own in-house prediction markets application, according to NPR.

Meta seriously considered acquiring Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction market platform, before pivoting to build a proprietary forecasting product internally, according to NPR. The revelation sheds light on how seriously major tech platforms are evaluating the prediction market space as a viable consumer product category.

Kalshi, which secured a landmark legal victory allowing it to offer U.S. political event contracts, has become one of the most prominent regulated prediction market venues in the country. A Meta acquisition would have represented a dramatic consolidation, bringing a federally licensed exchange under the roof of one of the world's largest social media companies.

Meta's eventual decision to build its own app rather than acquire Kalshi suggests the company saw sufficient opportunity to compete from scratch, potentially leveraging its massive user base. The move also signals growing mainstream corporate interest in prediction markets as an engagement and information product beyond niche trading audiences.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, Meta entering the space — whether via acquisition or organic build — could dramatically expand liquidity and user volume across the sector, lifting all boats or intensifying competition. Regulators at the CFTC will likely face new pressure to clarify rules around who can operate event-contract platforms, especially if a social media giant enters. Kalshi's valuation and strategic positioning may also shift as rivals now know it was deemed acquisition-worthy by Meta.
