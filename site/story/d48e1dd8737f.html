Prediction Market Daily Volume Jumps 80% in June, Sports Dominates

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 10:03 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Prediction market daily trading volume surged 80% in June, with sports-related contracts driving nearly nine-tenths of all activity, according to a new Jefferies analysis.

Jefferies research found that daily trading volume across prediction markets climbed 80% in June compared to prior periods, signaling a significant acceleration in retail and institutional engagement with event contracts. The investment bank's analysis, as reported by Casino.org, highlights how the sector is moving well beyond its political-betting roots.

Sports contracts accounted for 89% of total daily volume in June, according to Casino.org's coverage of the Jefferies report. That concentration underscores how sports-event contracts have become the dominant commercial engine for platforms competing in the regulated prediction-market space, outpacing political and macro-economic contract categories by a wide margin.

The findings arrive as platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket compete aggressively for sports-betting adjacent audiences, and as the CFTC continues to navigate its regulatory posture toward event contracts. Sustained volume growth strengthens the commercial case for federally regulated prediction markets and may intensify pressure on traditional sportsbooks operating under state licensing regimes.

Why this matters

An 80% volume spike gives regulators concrete data on market scale, potentially accelerating CFTC rulemaking timelines for event contracts. For traders, sports-heavy volume means tighter spreads and deeper liquidity in those markets but thinner books elsewhere. Platform operators will likely respond by expanding sports contract offerings, further blurring the line between prediction markets and regulated sports wagering.
