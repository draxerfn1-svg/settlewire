ESMA: Most EU Prediction Market Event Contracts Already Banned for Retail

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 16:54 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Europe's top securities regulator has warned that a large share of prediction market event contracts are already prohibited for retail investors under existing EU law.

The European Securities and Markets Authority issued a warning clarifying that many event contracts traded on prediction market platforms likely fall under current EU financial regulations that restrict or outright ban their sale to retail customers, according to TradingView's coverage of the ESMA statement. The regulator did not announce new rules but stressed that existing frameworks already apply.

ESMA's position signals that platforms operating in Europe — or serving European retail users — may already be in violation of rules such as MiFID II or EMIR without awaiting any new legislation. The warning puts the compliance burden squarely on operators, who must assess whether their contracts qualify as financial instruments under EU definitions.

The development adds regulatory pressure to a sector that has been expanding rapidly. Platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi have attracted growing user bases globally, but Europe has long been a more legally fraught environment. ESMA's clarification suggests the window for operating in a gray zone may be closing faster than many operators anticipated.

Why this matters

Traders routing volume through European accounts or VPNs face real counterparty and withdrawal risk if platforms are forced to restrict EU access. Operators must now conduct immediate legal audits or risk enforcement actions, which could fragment liquidity across global prediction markets. Regulation watchers should track whether ESMA follows this warning with formal guidance or referrals to national regulators.
