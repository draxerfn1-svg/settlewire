Polymarket Opens U.S. Marketing Push After Regulatory Thaw

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 18:04 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket has launched its first formal U.S.-facing marketing campaign, marking a significant strategic shift toward domestic customer acquisition following years of blocking American users.

Polymarket, the leading crypto-based prediction market platform, has initiated a U.S. marketing campaign, according to Yahoo Finance, signaling an aggressive pivot toward the American market it had previously been barred from serving. The move comes after Polymarket settled with the CFTC in January 2022 for $1.4 million over operating an unregistered facility, which at the time forced it to geo-block U.S. users.

The campaign represents a notable reversal in posture for the platform, which processed billions of dollars in trading volume during the 2024 U.S. presidential election cycle — almost entirely from non-U.S. participants. Polymarket's 2024 election markets drew mainstream media attention and significant liquidity, reportedly peaking at tens of millions of dollars in open interest on single contracts.

The timing aligns with a broader regulatory thaw under the current administration, which has signaled a more permissive stance toward crypto and event-contract platforms. Rival Kalshi, which secured federal authorization to operate legally in the U.S., has already begun marketing prediction markets to American retail audiences, raising competitive pressure on Polymarket to establish domestic brand recognition.

Why this matters

A U.S.-accessible Polymarket would dramatically expand the addressable liquidity pool for event contracts, potentially moving market prices closer to true consensus probabilities. Prediction-market traders should watch whether this campaign implies pending regulatory approval or a legal workaround, since either path sets a precedent for platform compliance models. Regulators and competitors alike will scrutinize how Polymarket navigates CFTC jurisdiction, as the outcome could redraw the competitive map between offshore and domestically licensed operators.
