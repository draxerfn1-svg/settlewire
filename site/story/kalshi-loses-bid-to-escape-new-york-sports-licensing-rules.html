Kalshi Loses Bid to Escape New York Sports Licensing Rules

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 21:45 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A New York court ruled against Kalshi, finding the prediction-markets platform cannot avoid the state's sports licensing requirements, in a decision that could reshape how event-contract operators navigate state oversight.

The ruling, reported by Newsday, determined that Kalshi's federally regulated status as a designated contract market does not shield it from New York's sports wagering licensing framework. Kalshi had argued its CFTC oversight preempted state-level requirements, a position the court rejected.

The decision marks a significant setback for Kalshi, which has been aggressively expanding its sports-event contracts since a federal appeals court cleared those markets in late 2024. The company had positioned its federal regulatory umbrella as sufficient authority to operate nationally without individual state licensing hurdles.

The ruling adds New York — one of the largest sports-betting markets in the country, generating over $2 billion in annual handle — to the list of jurisdictions where Kalshi must contend with state regulators. How the company responds, whether through appeal or licensing compliance, will set a precedent for rivals like Polymarket and ForecastEx eyeing similar expansions.

Why this matters

This decision directly tests the core legal theory underpinning Kalshi's growth strategy: that CFTC designation grants nationwide preemption over state gambling laws. Prediction-market traders should watch whether other high-population states move quickly to assert similar jurisdiction, which could fragment liquidity across platforms. Regulation watchers should note that courts are beginning to draw a cleaner line between federal event-contract authority and state consumer-protection frameworks.
