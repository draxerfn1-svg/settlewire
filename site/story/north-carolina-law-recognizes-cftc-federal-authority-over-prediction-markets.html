North Carolina law recognizes CFTC federal authority over prediction markets

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 05:51 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

North Carolina enacted legislation formally acknowledging the CFTC's preemptive federal regulatory authority over prediction markets, making it one of the first U.S. states to codify that distinction into state law.

North Carolina's legislature passed a bill explicitly recognizing that the Commodity Futures Trading Commission holds primary regulatory jurisdiction over prediction markets operating under federal event-contract rules. The move signals that state-level gambling or gaming statutes should not be applied to CFTC-regulated platforms, a legal boundary that has been contested across multiple states in recent years.

The legislation is particularly significant given ongoing battles between state attorneys general and platforms such as Kalshi and Polymarket, which have argued that federal commodities law shields them from state gaming enforcement. By codifying CFTC preemption, North Carolina effectively removes its own regulators as a potential threat to federally licensed prediction-market operators doing business with residents of the state.

The bill follows a broader national pattern: after the CFTC greenlighted election contracts in 2024, several jurisdictions began reconsidering how state law intersects with federally sanctioned event contracts. North Carolina's action could serve as a legislative template for other states seeking clarity, according to reporting by The Block and Decrypt.

Why this matters

State-by-state legal uncertainty has been a persistent operational risk for prediction-market platforms, and North Carolina's explicit preemption language reduces one jurisdiction's threat surface for platforms like Kalshi. Traders should watch whether other states follow suit, as a wave of similar laws would materially expand the addressable U.S. market for regulated event contracts. Conversely, states that push back could create a patchwork that keeps compliance costs high and constrains liquidity growth.
