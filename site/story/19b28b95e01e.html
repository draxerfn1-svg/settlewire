CFTC Proposes Rules Banning Injury and Prop Bets on Prediction Markets

Published JUN 10, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The CFTC has released a formal rulemaking proposal that would prohibit injury-related and proposition-style contracts on regulated prediction market platforms.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission put forward new proposed regulations that would draw clearer boundaries around what kinds of event contracts can legally trade on prediction market platforms, according to ESPN. The proposal specifically targets injury-related outcomes and proposition-style bets, categories the agency appears eager to wall off from the regulated event-contract space.

The move represents one of the most concrete regulatory actions the CFTC has taken since prediction markets like Kalshi and Polymarket surged in public visibility during the 2024 election cycle. By naming specific contract types it wants excluded, the agency is signaling it intends to actively shape — not merely oversee — what these platforms can list, according to ESPN's coverage of the proposal.

The proposal arrives as multiple platforms are racing to expand their contract offerings into sports and entertainment territory. A formal rule restricting injury props would directly constrain product roadmaps, particularly for operators who have been eyeing NFL and NBA season contracts as a major growth driver following Kalshi's legal victories over the CFTC on election markets.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, banned contract categories mean reduced liquidity pools and fewer hedging instruments in sports-adjacent markets. Platform operators face compliance redesigns and potential product rollbacks if the rule is finalized as proposed. Regulation watchers should note this as the CFTC shifting from reactive litigation to proactive rulemaking — a more durable and harder-to-challenge form of oversight.
