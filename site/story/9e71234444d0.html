Ohio AG Pushes CFTC to Cede Sports Market Oversight to States

Published APR 30, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Ohio Attorney General Dave Yost formally urged the CFTC to acknowledge that states, not federal regulators, hold primary jurisdiction over sports-based prediction market contracts.

Ohio Attorney General Dave Yost formally urged the CFTC to acknowledge that states, not federal regulators, hold primary jurisdiction over sports-based prediction market contracts.

Why this matters

If the CFTC accepts this argument, federally designated contract markets like Kalshi could face a patchwork of 50 different state licensing and compliance regimes for sports event contracts, dramatically raising operating costs. Prediction-market traders should watch for potential product pullbacks or geo-blocks in states that move to assert aggressive oversight before federal rules are finalized.
