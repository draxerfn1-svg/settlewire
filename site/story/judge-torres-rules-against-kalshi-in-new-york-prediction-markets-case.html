Judge Torres Rules Against Kalshi in New York Prediction Markets Case

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 06:14 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Federal Judge Analisa Torres, known for her Ripple/XRP ruling, dealt Kalshi a significant legal defeat in a New York court case tied to the company's prediction markets operations.

Judge Analisa Torres — the same federal jurist whose Ripple vs. SEC ruling reshaped crypto securities law — issued a ruling against Kalshi in a New York prediction markets case, according to Yahoo Finance. The decision represents a notable setback for the CFTC-regulated exchange, which has been aggressively expanding its event-contract offerings since winning its Washington D.C. regulatory battle.

Kalshi secured a landmark federal appeals court victory in 2024 that allowed it to offer congressional-control contracts, positioning itself as the leading regulated U.S. prediction market. This New York ruling, however, introduces fresh legal friction for the company as it scales into new contract categories including sports and elections, markets that have drawn scrutiny from both state regulators and federal overseers.

The involvement of Judge Torres adds symbolic weight to the decision given her profile in the financial-regulation space. The specific legal grounds and scope of the ruling were not fully detailed in available source material, but the loss signals that Kalshi's courtroom momentum is not universal and that state-level legal exposure remains a live variable for the platform.

Why this matters

A New York state-level legal loss for Kalshi could embolden other jurisdictions to challenge the company's contracts, creating regulatory fragmentation that traders must price into event-contract liquidity. Prediction-market operators watching Kalshi's expansion playbook now have a counterexample to weigh against its 2024 D.C. appeals win. For CFTC regulation watchers, the ruling raises questions about whether federal preemption arguments will hold as Kalshi pushes into politically sensitive contract categories.
