Roosevelt Institute: Kalshi retail users lost $500M since launch

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 19:03 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A Roosevelt Institute analysis finds that ordinary retail users on Kalshi have collectively lost approximately $500 million since the prediction-market platform launched, highlighting steep losses for everyday participants.

According to the Roosevelt Institute, retail traders on Kalshi have shed roughly $500 million in aggregate since the platform went live, a figure that underscores the asymmetry between professional market-makers and casual participants in regulated event contracts. The report frames these losses as a systemic outcome rather than isolated bad bets.

The findings arrive as Kalshi — one of the few CFTC-designated contract markets for event contracts — has aggressively expanded its product slate to cover elections, economics, and sports. The Roosevelt Institute's analysis suggests that the platform's growth has largely benefited sophisticated counterparties who consistently sit on the winning side of retail flow.

The report is likely to intensify scrutiny of whether existing CFTC oversight adequately protects retail participants in prediction markets, a question that has shadowed the industry since Kalshi's landmark legal victory cleared the way for election contracts in the United States.

Why this matters

A $500 million retail-loss figure gives regulators and lawmakers concrete ammunition to revisit disclosure and suitability requirements for CFTC-regulated event contracts. Traders watching Kalshi contract volumes should note that sustained retail losses could trigger user attrition or new onboarding restrictions that thin liquidity. If the Roosevelt Institute's framing gains traction in Washington, competitors like Polymarket may also face heightened pressure to demonstrate retail-protection mechanisms.
