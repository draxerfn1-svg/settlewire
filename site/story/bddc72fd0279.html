Kalshi Sues Illinois Over Sports Betting Tax on Prediction Markets

Published JUL 01, 2026 · 18:46 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi is challenging Illinois's application of its sports betting tax to prediction market contracts, escalating a nationwide fight over how event contracts should be regulated.

Kalshi has filed a legal challenge against Illinois over the state's attempt to apply its sports betting tax to the company's federally regulated prediction market contracts, according to PYMNTS.com. The dispute centers on whether Illinois has the authority to impose state gambling levies on a product that operates under federal CFTC oversight.

The case represents a direct collision between state gambling regulators and the federal framework that governs event contracts. Kalshi has consistently argued that its products are commodity contracts, not sports wagers, and therefore fall exclusively under federal jurisdiction — a position the company is now pressing through litigation in one of the country's largest sports-betting markets.

The Illinois confrontation is part of a broader pattern: as prediction markets expand into sports outcomes and elections, state gaming authorities have increasingly moved to assert jurisdiction. Similar jurisdictional questions have surfaced in other states, making this case a potential bellwether for whether federal preemption shields CFTC-regulated operators from state-level gambling taxes and licensing demands.

Why this matters

A ruling favoring Kalshi would create a strong federal-preemption precedent, directly limiting states' ability to tax or restrict other prediction-market operators like ForecastEx. Conversely, an Illinois victory could force Kalshi and rivals to navigate a patchwork of state tax regimes, significantly raising compliance costs and dampening market liquidity. Traders and operators should watch this case as a leading indicator of whether the prediction-market model scales nationally.
