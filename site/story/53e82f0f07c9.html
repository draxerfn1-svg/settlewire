CFTC Proposes Formal Rules for Prediction Market Public Interest Reviews

Published JUN 10, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The CFTC has issued a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking that would establish a structured framework for determining whether event contracts serve the public interest.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission released a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking aimed at codifying how it evaluates prediction market event contracts under the public interest standard, according to Davis Wright Tremaine. The move signals a shift from ad hoc reviews toward a more predictable regulatory process for platforms seeking to list event-based contracts.

Under current rules, the CFTC has broad discretion to block event contracts it deems contrary to the public interest, a power it has invoked in high-profile disputes with platforms like Kalshi over political event contracts. The proposed rulemaking would define criteria and procedures that could constrain that discretion and give operators clearer guidance before launching new contract types.

The prediction markets industry has grown substantially, with platforms such as Kalshi and Polymarket handling hundreds of millions of dollars in volume on political, economic, and sports outcomes. Regulatory clarity has been a persistent bottleneck for expansion, and a formal rulemaking process could accelerate both product development and institutional participation in the sector.

Why this matters

A codified public interest standard reduces the CFTC's ability to block novel contracts arbitrarily, which could open the door for contested contract categories — including political and sports markets — to gain approval more readily. Traders should watch for comment-period outcomes that may signal which contract types regulators are inclined to approve or restrict. Operators planning new product lines now have a formal channel to shape the rules before they are finalized.
