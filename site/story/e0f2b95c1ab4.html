Minnesota's Prediction Market Ban Takes Effect August 1

Published JUL 05, 2026 · 11:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Minnesota becomes the first U.S. state to ban prediction markets when its new law takes effect August 1, setting a precedent that could ripple nationally.

According to the Star Tribune, Minnesota's prohibition on prediction markets marks a historic first among U.S. states, with the law kicking in on August 1. The ban targets platforms that allow users to wager on the outcomes of real-world events, a category that has grown dramatically in recent years following federal regulatory shifts.

The Star Tribune framed the future of the industry as deeply uncertain, suggesting the state-level action could embolden other legislatures to follow suit. Prediction market operators like Kalshi and Polymarket have been expanding aggressively after the CFTC relaxed its posture toward event contracts, making Minnesota's move a notable counter-current to that federal permissiveness.

The tension between state and federal authority over prediction markets is now a live legal question. If federal regulators explicitly preempt state bans — or decline to — the outcome will define how broadly these platforms can operate across the country, potentially fragmenting the market into a patchwork of access rules.

Why this matters

A state-level ban creates immediate compliance obligations for Kalshi, Polymarket, and similar platforms, forcing geofencing of Minnesota users and raising operational costs. If other states replicate the model, the prediction-market industry could face a balkanized regulatory landscape that undermines liquidity and national growth. Traders should watch whether federal preemption arguments are raised in court, as a ruling either way would be a major valuation signal for the sector.
