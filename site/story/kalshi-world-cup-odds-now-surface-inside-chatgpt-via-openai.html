Kalshi World Cup odds now surface inside ChatGPT via OpenAI

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 03:43 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

OpenAI has begun displaying Kalshi's prediction-market odds for the FIFA World Cup directly within ChatGPT, giving the regulated event-contract exchange a high-visibility distribution channel for the first time.

ChatGPT is now surfacing real-money probability markets from Kalshi when users ask about World Cup outcomes, according to both The New York Times and Cointelegraph. The integration marks the first known instance of a CFTC-regulated prediction market being embedded inside a major AI assistant, exposing Kalshi's event contracts to ChatGPT's hundreds of millions of users.

Kalshi, which won a landmark legal battle against the CFTC in 2024 to offer political event contracts, has been aggressively expanding its product line into sports. The World Cup — scheduled for North America in 2026 — is among its highest-profile sports offerings. Showing live market-implied probabilities inside ChatGPT could drive both brand recognition and new depositors to the platform.

The move mirrors how financial data providers like Bloomberg or Refinitiv license odds and prices to third-party surfaces, but the AI-chat context is novel for prediction markets. Neither OpenAI nor Kalshi has publicly detailed the commercial terms of the arrangement, according to Cointelegraph.

Why this matters

Distribution inside a dominant AI interface could compress Kalshi's customer-acquisition costs dramatically, making this a potential moat that rivals like Polymarket — operating without CFTC approval — cannot easily replicate. Traders should watch whether the integration shifts liquidity and open interest toward Kalshi's World Cup contracts as casual users convert to active participants. Regulators may also scrutinize whether AI-surfaced odds constitute advertising subject to CFTC disclosure rules.
