Kalshi Hits $31B June Volume as World Cup Drives Record Trade

Published JUL 05, 2026 · 22:43 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi set a new monthly trading record of $31 billion in June, fueled by World Cup event contracts even as multiple U.S. states moved to criminalize its activity.

Kalshi recorded $31 billion in trading volume during June, marking the platform's highest monthly figure to date, according to Tech Times. The surge was largely attributed to heavy wagering on World Cup outcomes, demonstrating how major international sporting events can dramatically spike activity on regulated U.S. prediction markets.

The milestone arrives against a complicated legal backdrop. Tech Times reports that a growing number of U.S. states have introduced or enacted legislation classifying Kalshi-style event contracts as illegal gambling, with some statutes carrying felony-level penalties. The tension between federal CFTC authorization and state-level prohibition remains unresolved and is intensifying.

Kalshi holds federal approval from the CFTC to offer event contracts, giving it a legal foundation that competing offshore platforms like Polymarket lack in the U.S. market. However, the patchwork of state felony bans creates meaningful friction for user acquisition, platform expansion, and the broader legitimacy of the event-contract category.

Why this matters

A $31B monthly volume print signals that prediction markets are reaching mainstream liquidity levels, which matters deeply for market makers and traders pricing thin-margin contracts. The simultaneous state felony-ban wave, however, creates a fragmentation risk that could suppress user growth and push traders toward unregulated offshore venues. Regulation watchers should monitor whether the CFTC or Congress moves to preempt state prohibitions, as that outcome would be a watershed for the entire sector.
