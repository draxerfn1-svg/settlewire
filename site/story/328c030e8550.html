Cboe Seeks SEC Blessing for Earnings-Based Event Contracts

Published JUL 02, 2026 · 12:03 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Cboe has formally asked the SEC to approve a new class of event contracts tied to corporate earnings outcomes, expanding the regulated event-contract landscape beyond the CFTC's domain.

Cboe Global Markets has filed a request with the Securities and Exchange Commission seeking approval to offer event contracts based on corporate earnings results, according to Casino.org. The move would mark a significant expansion of exchange-traded event contracts into SEC-regulated territory, where earnings data is considered securities-adjacent information.

The application represents a direct challenge — or complement — to the CFTC-regulated event contract space currently dominated by platforms like Kalshi and ForecastEx. By routing earnings-based contracts through the SEC rather than the CFTC, Cboe appears to be carving out a separate regulatory pathway that could sidestep ongoing debates about what qualifies as a commodity-based event contract.

Earnings event contracts would allow traders to take positions on whether a company beats, meets, or misses analyst forecasts — essentially turning quarterly reporting season into a tradeable event market. The product would compete with existing prediction-market activity on platforms like Polymarket, where informal markets on corporate outcomes already attract significant volume.

Why this matters

If the SEC approves Cboe's application, it would establish a second major regulatory framework for event contracts alongside the CFTC, complicating jurisdiction boundaries and potentially triggering turf disputes between the two agencies. Kalshi and ForecastEx would face a well-capitalized incumbent competitor with established institutional distribution. Traders should watch for whether SEC approval sets a precedent that accelerates or restricts event-contract product development across the industry.
