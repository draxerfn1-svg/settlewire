Rothera picks Adaptive to build CFTC-regulated event contracts exchange

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 12:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Rothera has selected Adaptive's Aeron Exchange Accelerator technology to construct a CFTC-regulated event contracts trading and clearing platform, adding a new entrant to the regulated prediction-market space.

Rothera has chosen Adaptive Financial Consulting's Aeron Exchange Accelerator as the foundation for its forthcoming CFTC-regulated event contracts exchange, according to Business Wire. The platform will handle both trading and clearing functions, positioning Rothera as a direct competitor to established players like Kalshi and ForecastEx in the regulated U.S. event-contract market.

Adaptive's Aeron Exchange Accelerator is a low-latency, high-throughput exchange infrastructure product designed for derivatives markets. By licensing this technology, Rothera avoids building exchange plumbing from scratch, potentially accelerating its path to CFTC designation as a Designated Contract Market (DCM) or Swap Execution Facility — the regulatory hurdles that have defined the sector's competitive landscape.

The regulated event-contracts space has grown sharply since Kalshi won its landmark 2024 legal battle against the CFTC, opening the door for election and political contracts. New entrants like Rothera signal that institutional infrastructure providers and exchange operators see durable commercial opportunity in the category, intensifying competition for market share and liquidity.

Why this matters

A new CFTC-regulated exchange entrant backed by professional exchange infrastructure raises competitive pressure on Kalshi and ForecastEx, potentially fragmenting liquidity and compressing margins. Traders should watch whether Rothera pursues unique contract categories that could shift volume away from incumbents. Regulators will face growing demand to streamline DCM application processes as the pipeline of applicants widens.
