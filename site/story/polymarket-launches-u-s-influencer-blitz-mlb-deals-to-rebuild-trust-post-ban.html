Polymarket launches U.S. influencer blitz, MLB deals to rebuild trust post-ban

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 15:58 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket announced a broad U.S. marketing campaign on July 8, 2026, including TikTok influencer deals, Major League Baseball partnerships, and media agreements with CNN and CNBC as it works to re-establish credibility following a four-year domestic ban.

Dan Lee, Polymarket's head of U.S. operations, confirmed to the Associated Press that the platform is actively courting policymakers, regulators, and new users through a coordinated legitimacy push. The campaign spans social media influencer activations on TikTok, signed agreements with major sports franchises and MLB, and broadcast media partnerships with CNN and CNBC. Polymarket's X account now stands at 1.7 million followers, dwarfing rival Kalshi's 431,400.

The re-entry effort began in December 2025 when Polymarket launched a CFTC-supervised mobile app allowing U.S. residents to wager real money on sports outcomes. Lee acknowledged to AP that the company's dominant international trading volume has obscured its domestic progress, noting the U.S. push is aimed at broadening mainstream acceptance rather than simply recapturing existing crypto-native users.

The marketing offensive arrives under a cloud: the Wall Street Journal reported last month that Polymarket had paid influencers to promote simulated trades and fabricated winnings without adequate disclosure. Polymarket told the WSJ it remains 'committed to maintaining accurate, fair, and transparent markets.' That controversy sets a difficult backdrop for a campaign whose central message is trustworthiness, according to CoinDesk.

Why this matters

Prediction-market traders should watch whether Polymarket's MLB and media deals drive meaningful U.S. liquidity into its event contracts, which could compress spreads and shift volume away from Kalshi. The undisclosed-influencer controversy creates regulatory exposure at exactly the moment Polymarket needs CFTC goodwill to expand its supervised product lineup. For regulation watchers, how the CFTC responds to the disclosure allegations will signal how much latitude it is willing to grant newly re-entering platforms.
