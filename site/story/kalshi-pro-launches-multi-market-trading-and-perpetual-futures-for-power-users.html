Kalshi Pro launches multi-market trading and perpetual futures for power users

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 11:36 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi launched a new 'Pro' product, reported by CNBC, giving sophisticated users the ability to trade multiple prediction markets simultaneously alongside perpetual futures contracts.

Kalshi's new Pro tier marks the platform's most significant product expansion since winning its CFTC legal battle, according to CNBC. The offering bundles simultaneous multi-market trading — letting users manage positions across several event contracts at once — with perpetual futures, instruments that have no expiration date and are common in crypto derivatives markets but novel in regulated U.S. event-contract trading.

Perpetual futures on a CFTC-regulated venue like Kalshi represent a meaningful structural shift for the prediction-markets industry. Traditional event contracts resolve on a fixed date tied to a real-world outcome; perpetuals allow traders to hold leveraged directional exposure indefinitely, more closely mirroring the mechanics of established crypto exchanges such as Binance or Bybit.

The Pro launch signals Kalshi's intent to compete for the high-volume, sophisticated trader segment currently dominated by offshore crypto-native platforms like Polymarket, which runs on blockchain infrastructure outside direct U.S. jurisdiction. By packaging institutional-grade tools within a regulated wrapper, Kalshi is positioning itself as the compliant alternative for professional event-contract traders.

Why this matters

The introduction of perpetual futures on a CFTC-regulated exchange sets a regulatory precedent that could force the commission to clarify margin and position-limit rules for open-ended event contracts — a major variable for traders pricing regulatory risk. For prediction-market operators, Kalshi Pro raises the competitive bar: platforms without multi-market execution tools may struggle to retain high-frequency traders who generate disproportionate liquidity. Traders on political and economic markets should watch whether perpetual products attract new arbitrage flows that compress spreads on Kalshi's existing binary contracts.
