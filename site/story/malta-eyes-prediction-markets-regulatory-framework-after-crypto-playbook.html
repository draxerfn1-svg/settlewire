Malta Eyes Prediction Markets Regulatory Framework After Crypto Playbook

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 12:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Malta is pursuing a formal regulatory framework for prediction markets, applying lessons from its earlier cryptocurrency licensing push to attract prediction-market operators to the jurisdiction.

According to Finance Magnates, Malta is drawing on its experience establishing crypto regulation under the Virtual Financial Assets Act to craft rules specifically targeting prediction markets. The island nation became known as 'Blockchain Island' after aggressively courting crypto firms around 2018, and officials are now signaling a similar ambition for event-contract platforms.

Malta's approach would position it as a European hub for prediction-market operators seeking regulatory clarity — a scarce commodity globally, given that the CFTC in the United States continues to litigate boundaries around platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket. A defined Maltese license could offer operators a compliant base to serve EU users under MiCA-adjacent structures.

The timing matters: prediction markets saw explosive volume growth through 2024's election cycle, and platforms are actively scouting jurisdictions. Finance Magnates notes that Malta's MGA and MFSA are the likely regulatory bodies involved, though a formal framework has not yet been published.

Why this matters

A recognized EU jurisdiction entering this space would give prediction-market operators a credible offshore-but-regulated option, reducing dependence on CFTC approval and potentially accelerating global expansion. Traders on platforms like Polymarket — currently operating in a legal grey zone for U.S. users — would gain access to legitimized venues. Regulation watchers should track whether Malta's framework influences CFTC thinking on event contracts, as international competition has historically pressured U.S. regulators to act.
