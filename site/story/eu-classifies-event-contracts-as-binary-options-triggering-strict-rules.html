EU Classifies Event Contracts as Binary Options, Triggering Strict Rules

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 22:25 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

European regulators have formally classified event contracts as binary options under EU law, subjecting prediction-market platforms to the bloc's strict binary-options regulatory framework.

The European Union's classification of event contracts as binary options immediately brings platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi — should they operate in the EU — under MiFID II and ESMA's existing binary-options rules, which have effectively banned retail binary-options products in Europe since 2018. The designation was reported by TradingView in its weekly wrap coverage.

ESMA's 2018 binary-options retail ban was introduced after regulators identified widespread mis-selling and investor harm. By slotting event contracts into that same legal category, EU authorities are signaling that prediction-market products offering fixed yes/no payouts share enough structural similarity with traditional binary options to warrant identical oversight — a finding that could freeze expansion by US-based platforms into European markets.

The regulatory move arrives as Kalshi and Polymarket are both scaling aggressively following US legal wins. Any EU market-entry strategy must now account for either obtaining a full MiFID II license and navigating the retail ban, or restructuring contracts so they fall outside the binary-options definition — both costly paths.

Why this matters

Prediction-market operators eyeing European expansion now face the same regulatory wall that killed retail binary-options brokers in the EU, making near-term EU launches by Kalshi or Polymarket far less likely. Traders should watch whether platforms attempt to reframe contract structures to avoid the binary-options label, as product design pivots could affect liquidity and payout mechanics. Regulation watchers should note this as a precedent that could influence how other jurisdictions — Canada, Australia, UK — choose to classify event contracts going forward.
