Kalshi secures $30M investment from ETF XOVR in prediction market bet

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 12:44 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

ETF issuer XOVR committed $30 million to prediction market exchange Kalshi as a private market investment, expanding its strategy of backing high-growth alternative asset firms.

XOVR, the ETF issuer previously known for a $30 million private stake in SpaceX, has now directed an equivalent sum into Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange, according to PR Newswire and InvestmentNews. The move signals a deliberate pattern by XOVR of targeting pre-IPO or private companies operating at the frontier of financial innovation, using ETF structures to give retail investors indirect exposure.

Kalshi, which secured CFTC approval to operate event contracts in the United States, has rapidly grown its product suite to include markets on elections, economic indicators, and weather events. The $30 million infusion adds institutional credibility to the platform at a moment when prediction markets are drawing significant mainstream attention following high-profile trading volumes during the 2024 U.S. election cycle.

XOVR's strategy mirrors a broader trend of asset managers packaging illiquid private stakes into exchange-traded products, allowing everyday investors to participate in sectors previously limited to venture capital. For Kalshi, the capital injection also arrives as competition with rivals like Polymarket intensifies and the regulatory landscape for event contracts continues to evolve under CFTC oversight.

Why this matters

A $30 million institutional investment specifically earmarked for Kalshi strengthens the exchange's balance sheet ahead of what could be a pivotal period for CFTC event-contract rulemaking. Prediction-market traders should note that increased capital backing typically accelerates product development, liquidity, and market-maker incentives. For regulation watchers, mainstream ETF involvement lends political legitimacy to the asset class at a time when its legal standing remains an active battleground.
