Polymarket Files for US Margin Trading License on Event Contracts

Published JUL 09, 2026 · 20:25 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket has submitted regulatory applications to offer margin trading on prediction-market contracts to US users, marking its most direct push yet toward legal domestic operations.

Polymarket filed applications seeking approval to provide leveraged, margin-based trading on event contracts to customers in the United States, according to reporting by Bloomberg.com and CoinDesk. The move represents a significant escalation from the platform's existing posture, under which US users have technically been excluded following a 2022 CFTC settlement in which Polymarket paid $1.4 million and agreed to block American participants.

The Block confirmed the filings, noting that Polymarket is pursuing a regulated pathway rather than continuing to operate in a legal gray zone. Margin trading on prediction markets would allow users to take positions larger than their deposited collateral, amplifying both potential gains and losses on outcomes ranging from elections to economic indicators.

The applications signal that Polymarket, which recorded over $500 million in monthly trading volume during peak 2024 election activity, is betting that the current regulatory environment — shaped partly by a more crypto-friendly posture in Washington — is favorable enough to seek formal US authorization rather than wait for clearer legislative guidance.

Why this matters

Approval would fundamentally reshape the US prediction-market landscape, giving Polymarket a major structural advantage over rivals like Kalshi and ForecastEx that have built regulated frameworks without offering leverage. Margin trading dramatically expands addressable volume and trader sophistication, which could pressure competitors to seek similar permissions quickly. Regulators' response will also set a precedent for whether event contracts are treated more like derivatives or retail betting products under US law.
