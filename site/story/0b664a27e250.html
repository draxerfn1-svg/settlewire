Democrats Press CFTC to Crack Down on Prediction Market Sports Contracts

Published APR 30, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A group of Democratic lawmakers has called on the CFTC to tighten oversight of prediction markets, specifically targeting sports-event contracts and potential insider trading risks.

Democratic members of Congress sent a formal letter to the Commodity Futures Trading Commission urging regulators to take a harder line on prediction markets that allow wagering on sporting event outcomes, according to CNBC. The lawmakers expressed concern that these products blur the line between regulated financial derivatives and unlicensed sports gambling.

The letter also flagged insider trading vulnerabilities unique to event contracts, where participants with privileged knowledge — such as team staff or athletes — could potentially profit from non-public information. According to CNBC, the Democrats argued the CFTC's current framework is ill-equipped to detect or deter that kind of activity in fast-moving event markets.

The intervention comes as platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket have rapidly expanded sports-related contract offerings following a string of legal and regulatory wins. The CFTC has been under mounting pressure from both industry players seeking clarity and critics who argue the agency has been too permissive in allowing products that resemble traditional sports betting.

Why this matters

Congressional pressure on the CFTC could accelerate rulemaking or guidance that directly limits the scope of sports contracts on platforms like Kalshi, squeezing a fast-growing revenue vertical. Traders with open positions on sports event contracts should watch for any emergency CFTC action or new proposed rules that could affect contract availability. Longer term, a formal crackdown could also create a regulatory wedge between US-based platforms and offshore operators like Polymarket, reshaping competitive dynamics.
