Burry bets on sportsbooks, predicts regulation will curb prediction markets

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 19:33 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Legendary investor Michael Burry disclosed long positions in DraftKings and Flutter in Q1 2025, publicly predicting that incoming regulation will constrain prediction-market platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket.

Michael Burry's Scion Asset Management revealed stakes in sportsbook giants DraftKings and Flutter Entertainment, according to CNBC, with Burry framing the trade as a direct contrast to his bearish outlook on prediction markets. He believes regulatory pressure will limit the growth of event-contract platforms, making licensed sportsbooks the safer long-term bet for capturing retail gambling demand.

Burry's thesis centers on the CFTC's ongoing scrutiny of platforms such as Kalshi and Polymarket, which have expanded aggressively into sports and political event contracts. If regulators tighten the definition of permissible event contracts or impose stricter licensing requirements, prediction-market operators could face significant volume caps or outright restrictions on certain contract types.

The prediction-market industry has been riding a wave of post-2024-election momentum, with Polymarket reporting record volumes and Kalshi winning key legal battles against the CFTC. Burry's contrarian positioning signals that at least one prominent Wall Street figure believes the regulatory pendulum is about to swing back against the sector.

Why this matters

If Burry's regulatory thesis proves correct, traders holding positions on Kalshi or Polymarket growth markets face a meaningful downside catalyst that may not yet be priced in. A CFTC crackdown on sports event contracts would directly redirect liquidity toward licensed sportsbooks, validating Burry's DraftKings and Flutter longs as a hedge signal worth tracking. Regulation watchers should treat this as a high-profile sentiment indicator that institutional money is starting to fade the prediction-market expansion narrative.
