Ninth Circuit hears Kalshi tribal gaming classification dispute

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 10:47 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals took up a legal challenge examining whether Kalshi's event contracts constitute tribal gaming activity, in a case with sweeping implications for prediction market regulation.

The Ninth Circuit is now weighing arguments in a dispute that centers on whether Kalshi's federally regulated event contracts fall under tribal gaming statutes, according to ReadWrite. The case forces appellate judges to draw a legal line between CFTC-overseen prediction markets and gambling activities governed by the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act.

Kalshi, which won a landmark CFTC battle in 2024 to offer congressional election contracts, now faces a separate front in its legal war over how its products are classified. A tribal gaming classification could expose Kalshi and similar platforms to an entirely different regulatory regime, one administered outside the CFTC's jurisdiction.

The Ninth Circuit's geographic reach covers major U.S. financial and tech hubs, meaning a ruling here could set binding precedent across a large swath of the country. Competing platforms including Polymarket and ForecastEx would likely feel downstream effects if the court determines that event contracts overlap with gaming law.

Why this matters

A ruling that blurs the line between CFTC event contracts and tribal gaming could freeze market expansion plans and force traders to reprice regulatory risk on positions tied to U.S.-listed prediction platforms. If tribal gaming law applies, operators may need licenses from an entirely separate federal framework, dramatically raising compliance costs and barriers to entry. Prediction-market traders should watch this case as a leading indicator of whether the asset class remains under the CFTC's relatively permissive umbrella.
