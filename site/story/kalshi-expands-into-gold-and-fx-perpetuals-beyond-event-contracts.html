Kalshi Expands Into Gold and FX Perpetuals Beyond Event Contracts

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 10:53 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Prediction-market exchange Kalshi is moving beyond its core event-contract business by launching perpetual contracts in gold and foreign exchange markets, broadening its product suite.

Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange, is pushing into new territory with plans to offer perpetual contracts tied to gold prices and foreign-exchange pairs, according to Bitcoin Foundation reporting. The move represents a significant strategic pivot for a platform best known for letting users trade on yes/no outcomes of real-world events.

Perpetual contracts — derivatives with no fixed expiration date — are a staple of crypto trading venues but remain relatively rare on regulated U.S. exchanges. By adding gold and FX perps, Kalshi would be competing not just with fellow event-contract platforms like Polymarket and ForecastEx, but with a much wider universe of commodity and currency derivatives venues.

The expansion follows a period of rapid growth for Kalshi after court victories affirmed its right to offer politically themed contracts. Adding non-event derivatives suggests the company is leveraging its regulatory foothold to capture a broader slice of the derivatives market rather than remaining a niche prediction-market operator.

Why this matters

Kalshi entering gold and FX perpetuals signals that CFTC-licensed event-contract venues may evolve into full-spectrum derivatives exchanges, raising the competitive stakes for incumbents like CME and ICE. For prediction-market traders, this diversification could attract deeper liquidity pools that spill back into event contracts. Regulators will face fresh questions about whether the CFTC's event-contract framework is being stretched to cover instruments that look more like traditional leveraged derivatives.
