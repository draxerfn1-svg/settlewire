Kalshi wins right to block Michigan users by address, not geofence

Published JUL 12, 2026 · 17:38 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A court ruling allows Kalshi to enforce its Michigan user ban through address-based verification rather than GPS geofencing, a significant compliance method distinction for the prediction-markets platform.

According to LegalSportsReport, Kalshi secured a legal determination permitting it to block Michigan-based users via address verification rather than relying on geolocation fencing technology. The distinction matters operationally: address-based blocking is less technically burdensome and less prone to false positives from VPNs or border-area users.

Michigan has been a contested jurisdiction for Kalshi as the platform navigates a patchwork of state-level restrictions following its CFTC-regulated launch of event contracts. The ruling provides Kalshi a more flexible compliance pathway without requiring the real-time location infrastructure that sportsbooks and daily fantasy operators typically deploy.

The decision adds to a growing body of case law defining how federally regulated event-contract platforms must satisfy state exclusion requirements. Unlike sports betting operators who face clear state licensing regimes, prediction markets like Kalshi occupy a hybrid federal-state regulatory space that courts are still mapping in real time.

Why this matters

This ruling sets a precedent for how other CFTC-regulated prediction-market platforms — including potential Kalshi competitors — can structure geographic compliance without expensive geofencing infrastructure. Traders should watch whether Michigan pursues further action or whether this emboldens Kalshi to contest other state restrictions. Regulation watchers will note that address-verification as an acceptable standard could lower the barrier for new event-contract entrants navigating multi-state exclusion orders.
