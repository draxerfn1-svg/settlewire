Michigan Judge Halts Kalshi Sports Event Contracts

Published JUL 01, 2026 · 09:52 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A Michigan federal judge has issued a temporary block on Kalshi's sports-event contracts, dealing a significant legal blow to the prediction market platform.

A Michigan judge granted a temporary restraining order halting Kalshi from offering sports-event contracts in what amounts to a direct legal challenge to the platform's expanding sports markets, according to gamingintelligence.com. The ruling puts a pause on contracts tied to sporting outcomes that Kalshi had been operating under its CFTC-regulated framework.

Kalshi has argued that its event contracts are federally regulated derivatives, not sports wagers subject to state gambling laws — a legal theory that has been central to its growth strategy. The Michigan action suggests state authorities are willing to contest that argument in court, potentially setting a precedent for how other states treat federally sanctioned event contracts.

The case is part of a broader national tension between state gambling regulators and CFTC-regulated prediction markets. Several states have pushed back against Kalshi's sports contracts, viewing them as unlicensed sports betting operating under a regulatory loophole. The outcome of this Michigan proceeding could influence how aggressively other states pursue similar injunctions.

Why this matters

This temporary block is a critical inflection point for prediction-market traders and operators: if Michigan's restraining order is upheld or expanded, it could fragment Kalshi's user base by geography and force the CFTC into a direct confrontation with state gaming regulators. Operators watching the space should monitor whether other states file copycat actions, which would materially raise compliance costs and restrict contract availability. Traders holding open sports-event positions on Kalshi face potential forced settlement or restricted access depending on how quickly the legal challenge escalates.
