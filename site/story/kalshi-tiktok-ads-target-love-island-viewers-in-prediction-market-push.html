Kalshi TikTok ads target Love Island viewers in prediction market push

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 09:01 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi is running a wave of prediction-market betting advertisements on TikTok aimed at Love Island fans, according to Business Insider, marking an aggressive social-media push to recruit younger users.

Business Insider reports that Love Island viewers scrolling TikTok are encountering repeated Kalshi ads pitching event-contract wagering on the reality show, a strategy that places the CFTC-regulated platform squarely in the entertainment and pop-culture advertising space previously dominated by traditional sportsbooks.

Kalshi, which won a landmark legal battle against the CFTC in 2024 to offer sports and entertainment event contracts, has been expanding its product catalog aggressively since that ruling. Targeting a young, highly engaged reality-TV audience on TikTok signals the company is prioritizing top-of-funnel user acquisition at scale rather than relying on organic growth among finance-native users.

The campaign illustrates how prediction-market operators are beginning to compete directly with sports-betting apps for casual consumer attention. TikTok's algorithm-driven ad delivery makes it particularly effective for reaching 18-to-25-year-olds, a demographic both Kalshi and rival Polymarket have identified as core to long-term platform growth.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, rising user acquisition costs and mainstream ad campaigns suggest Kalshi is prioritizing volume and liquidity growth, which could tighten spreads on entertainment contracts. Regulators watching the space should note that consumer-facing TikTok ads dramatically widen the audience beyond sophisticated traders, potentially inviting fresh CFTC or FTC scrutiny around advertising standards. Competitors like Polymarket may face pressure to match Kalshi's marketing spend or risk ceding the casual-user segment entirely.
