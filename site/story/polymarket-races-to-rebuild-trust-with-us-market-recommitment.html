Polymarket Races to Rebuild Trust With US Market Recommitment

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 10:16 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket is mounting a campaign to restore credibility and re-enter the US prediction-market space after a period of regulatory uncertainty and reputational damage, according to reporting by AP News and Yahoo Finance.

Polymarket, the crypto-based prediction market platform, is actively working to win back user trust and re-establish itself as a legitimate player in the US market, according to AP News and Yahoo Finance. The move comes after the platform faced scrutiny over its prior ban on US users following a 2022 CFTC settlement in which Polymarket paid $1.4 million without admitting wrongdoing.

The recommitment to the US market represents a significant strategic pivot. With prediction markets gaining mainstream attention during the 2024 US election cycle — when Polymarket logged hundreds of millions of dollars in election-related trading volume — the platform appears eager to capitalize on elevated public awareness while competitors like Kalshi have already secured CFTC approval to operate domestically.

The trust-rebuilding effort involves signaling regulatory compliance readiness and broadening the platform's appeal to American users who were previously locked out. AP News and Yahoo Finance both characterized the initiative as high-stakes, underscoring the competitive and regulatory pressures Polymarket faces as the US event-contracts landscape rapidly matures.

Why this matters

Polymarket's US push directly intensifies competition with CFTC-regulated rivals like Kalshi and ForecastEx, potentially compressing market share for compliant incumbents. Traders should watch for any formal CFTC filing or licensing announcement as a hard catalyst for volume migration. Regulators will scrutinize whether Polymarket's re-entry plan includes full compliance infrastructure or tests existing jurisdictional boundaries.
