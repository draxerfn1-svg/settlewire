Kalshi develops event contracts tied to AI computing power markets

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 14:01 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi is expanding its prediction-market offerings to include contracts linked to AI computing power, ramping up efforts to build a new asset class around GPU and cloud capacity pricing.

Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange, is pushing into artificial intelligence infrastructure by developing markets tied to AI computing power, according to Bloomberg.com. The move represents a significant product expansion beyond the political and economic event contracts that originally defined the platform.

AI computing capacity — particularly GPU time sold through cloud providers — has become a volatile, high-demand commodity as model training costs surge. By structuring event contracts around pricing benchmarks or availability thresholds for this compute, Kalshi would give traders a regulated venue to express directional views on the AI infrastructure economy, Bloomberg.com reported.

The timing aligns with broader Wall Street interest in AI infrastructure as an investable theme. Kalshi's regulated status under the CFTC gives it a structural advantage over unregulated venues in offering institutional-grade contracts, potentially attracting hedgers such as AI startups and cloud buyers alongside speculative traders.

Why this matters

A liquid compute-power prediction market could become a genuine price-discovery tool for AI infrastructure costs, creating second-order demand from enterprises seeking to hedge GPU procurement expenses. For prediction-market traders, new non-political contract categories reduce correlated risk across a portfolio. Regulators will be watching closely to see whether CFTC event-contract rules stretch cleanly to commodity-adjacent AI benchmarks without requiring new rulemaking.
