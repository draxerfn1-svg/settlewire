Cboe targets prediction market users for options trading conversion

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 07:06 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Cboe Global Markets is actively pursuing prediction market participants as a pipeline for converting retail traders into options customers, the exchange operator revealed in a strategy disclosure reported by Traders Magazine.

Cboe Global Markets has identified prediction market traders as a high-value recruiting pool for its options business, according to Traders Magazine. The exchange giant views the growing retail audience on platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket as already primed for binary-style risk thinking, making them natural candidates for a transition to listed options products.

The strategic pivot reflects broader industry recognition that prediction markets have successfully onboarded a new generation of retail participants who are comfortable with probability-based wagering. Cboe, which operates the largest U.S. options exchange by volume, sees the binary payoff structure of event contracts as functionally analogous to certain options strategies, lowering the conceptual barrier for conversion.

Cboe has not disclosed specific conversion targets or timelines, but the initiative signals how traditional exchanges are now treating regulated prediction market platforms less as competitors and more as a top-of-funnel acquisition channel. Industry observers note this represents a significant legitimization of the event-contract sector by one of Wall Street's most established venue operators.

Why this matters

Cboe's explicit targeting of prediction market users as options recruits validates event contracts as a durable retail financial product rather than a regulatory experiment. For prediction market operators, this creates both a partnership opportunity and a competitive threat — a major exchange with deep pockets could build its own event-contract wrapper. Regulators watching CFTC-approved contracts may face new pressure to clarify the boundary between event contracts and options as the two markets deliberately converge.
