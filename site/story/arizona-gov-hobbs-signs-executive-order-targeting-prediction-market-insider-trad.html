Arizona Gov. Hobbs Signs Executive Order Targeting Prediction Market Insider Trading

Published JUL 09, 2026 · 17:35 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Arizona Governor Katie Hobbs issued an executive order targeting insider trading on prediction markets, making Arizona the first state to directly regulate information abuse on event-contract platforms.

Governor Katie Hobbs signed the executive order, according to KTAR News 92.3 FM, directing state agencies to address the use of non-public information to gain unfair advantages on prediction market platforms. The order represents a direct state-level intervention into a regulatory space that the federal CFTC has only partially addressed through its oversight of designated contract markets like Kalshi and ForecastEx.

The move comes as prediction markets have surged in mainstream visibility following the 2024 U.S. election cycle, during which platforms like Polymarket recorded hundreds of millions of dollars in political contract volume. Critics and regulators have raised concerns that participants with privileged access to information — such as campaign insiders or government officials — could exploit prediction markets for personal financial gain.

Arizona's action adds pressure on federal regulators to clarify insider trading rules specific to event contracts, a legal gray area that the CFTC has not fully resolved. State-level executive orders, while limited in jurisdictional reach, can set precedent and signal to Congress that legislative action on prediction market integrity may be warranted.

Why this matters

A state executive order targeting prediction market insider trading could accelerate federal rulemaking by the CFTC, forcing platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket to implement stricter user disclosure protocols. Traders operating in political or policy-linked markets face new legal exposure if they hold non-public government information. Operators should watch whether other states follow Arizona's lead, which could create a fragmented compliance landscape ahead of any unified federal framework.
