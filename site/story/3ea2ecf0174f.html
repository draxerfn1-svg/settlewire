New Jersey Takes Kalshi Sports Contract Fight to Supreme Court

Published JUL 02, 2026 · 13:53 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

New Jersey is preparing a Supreme Court petition challenging Kalshi's right to offer sports-event contracts, escalating a legal battle over federal versus state gambling authority.

New Jersey is moving to bring its dispute with Kalshi before the U.S. Supreme Court, according to Casino.org. The state is contesting whether Kalshi's CFTC-regulated sports event contracts can legally operate without state gambling oversight, a question that has divided lower courts and regulators.

The core legal tension centers on whether federal commodities law preempts state gambling statutes when it comes to event contracts on sporting outcomes. Kalshi has argued its products are legitimate CFTC-regulated derivatives, not sports wagers subject to state jurisdiction. New Jersey, home to a robust licensed sports-betting industry, disagrees and sees the contracts as an unlicensed incursion into its regulated market.

The outcome could reshape the competitive landscape for prediction-market operators nationwide. A Supreme Court ruling favorable to Kalshi would likely open the door for federally regulated event contracts to expand into sports outcomes across all 50 states, bypassing state licensing frameworks that traditional sportsbooks must navigate.

Why this matters

A Supreme Court ruling would set binding national precedent on whether CFTC-regulated event contracts can coexist with or supersede state gambling law — a decision that affects every prediction-market operator eyeing sports contracts. Traders on platforms like Polymarket should watch closely, as the ruling could either accelerate or permanently close U.S. sports event-contract markets. Regulators and legislators would face pressure to redraw the boundary between derivatives law and gambling law regardless of which side prevails.
