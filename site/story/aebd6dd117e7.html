Judge Weighs Minnesota's Bid to Ban Prediction Markets

Published JUL 02, 2026 · 19:51 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A federal judge is considering whether Minnesota can enforce its ban on prediction markets after Kalshi and Polymarket filed suit to block the state law.

A closely watched legal battle over state authority to regulate prediction markets landed before a federal judge, with Kalshi and Polymarket jointly challenging Minnesota's prohibition on event contracts, according to MPR News and Courthouse News. The platforms argue that federal oversight through the CFTC preempts any state-level ban on their products.

The case centers on whether Minnesota's gambling statutes can be applied to federally regulated event contracts. Kalshi, which won a landmark federal court ruling in 2024 affirming its right to offer political event contracts, and Polymarket are arguing that states cannot impose separate restrictions on markets already operating under federal jurisdiction, according to Courthouse News.

The outcome carries national implications: several other states have signaled interest in restricting prediction markets, meaning a ruling in Minnesota's favor could trigger a wave of state-level crackdowns, while a win for the platforms could effectively shield them from state interference across the country.

Why this matters

A ruling for the platforms would establish a strong federal-preemption firewall, making it far harder for any state to block prediction-market activity and opening the door to broader retail participation. Conversely, a ruling upholding Minnesota's ban could fragment the U.S. market geographically, forcing platforms to implement state-by-state access controls and chilling liquidity. Traders and operators should watch this case as a leading indicator of the regulatory runway for U.S. event contracts.
