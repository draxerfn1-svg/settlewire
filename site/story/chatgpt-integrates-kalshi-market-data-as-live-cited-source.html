ChatGPT integrates Kalshi market data as live cited source

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 16:19 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

OpenAI's ChatGPT began citing Kalshi prediction-market data as a named source in responses, giving the platform direct visibility inside the world's most-used AI assistant.

ChatGPT is now surfacing Kalshi contract prices and probabilities as attributed data points within its answers, according to The Verge. The integration means users asking about election odds, economic outcomes, or other event probabilities may receive Kalshi-sourced figures directly inside a ChatGPT response, complete with a citation back to the platform.

Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated event-contract exchange, has been aggressively expanding its data distribution since winning its legal battle to offer election markets in 2024. Appearing inside ChatGPT — which commands hundreds of millions of users — represents a significant step beyond traditional financial data terminals and news aggregators as a distribution channel.

The move mirrors a broader trend of prediction-market platforms seeking mainstream legitimacy through technology integrations. Polymarket data has previously appeared in AI-adjacent tools and search features, intensifying the race among regulated and unregulated venues to become the canonical probability layer cited by AI systems.

Why this matters

AI citation is fast becoming a new form of market authority — if ChatGPT defaults to Kalshi probabilities, traders on rival platforms face a reflexivity problem where AI-amplified Kalshi prices may anchor public expectations. For regulation watchers, this raises questions about whether AI-distributed event-contract data triggers new disclosure or registration obligations. Prediction-market operators without an AI distribution deal now have a concrete competitive disadvantage to price in.
