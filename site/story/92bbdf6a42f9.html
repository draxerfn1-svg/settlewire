Michigan Orders Kalshi to Stop Taking Bets in State

Published JUL 01, 2026 · 01:55 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Michigan regulators have directed prediction-market platform Kalshi to cease operations within the state, marking a fresh legal challenge for the CFTC-regulated exchange.

According to WILX, Michigan authorities issued an order requiring Kalshi to halt its operations in the state. The move represents one of the most direct state-level confrontations with Kalshi since the platform secured federal regulatory approval to offer event contracts through the CFTC.

Kalshi has positioned itself as a federally licensed exchange, arguing that CFTC oversight preempts state gambling laws. Michigan's action suggests state regulators disagree, setting up a potential legal clash over whether federal authorization shields Kalshi from state-by-state enforcement actions.

The dispute echoes earlier battles Kalshi fought — and largely won — against the CFTC itself over election contracts. However, state preemption arguments are legally distinct and less settled terrain, meaning Michigan's order could open the door for other states to pursue similar crackdowns on event-contract platforms.

Why this matters

A successful Michigan enforcement action could embolden other states to issue their own halt orders, fragmenting Kalshi's U.S. user base and raising compliance costs significantly. For prediction-market traders, this introduces platform-access risk; for operators, it signals that federal licensure alone may not provide the legal cover they assumed. Regulation watchers should monitor whether Kalshi seeks a federal injunction, which could produce precedent-setting rulings on CFTC preemption of state gambling law.
