Davis Wright Tremaine: Insider Trading Risk Now Hits Prediction Markets

Published JUL 09, 2026 · 19:11 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Law firm Davis Wright Tremaine published a compliance alert warning that insider trading rules now materially apply to prediction markets, urging platforms and participants to update their programs immediately.

Davis Wright Tremaine, a national law firm with a prominent regulatory practice, issued guidance identifying prediction markets as a newly urgent frontier for insider trading exposure. The alert signals that as platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket grow in volume and legitimacy, regulators and prosecutors are increasingly likely to scrutinize trading activity for material non-public information violations.

The firm's analysis suggests that existing compliance frameworks — largely borrowed from traditional securities or commodities contexts — may be inadequate for event-contract venues. Participants who trade on contracts tied to elections, economic data releases, or corporate events could face liability if they possess information unavailable to the broader market, according to Davis Wright Tremaine's guidance.

The warning arrives as CFTC-designated contract markets, including ForecastEx and Kalshi, operate under federal oversight, making the regulatory stakes concrete. Unlike informal offshore platforms, regulated U.S. prediction markets carry the full weight of federal commodities law, meaning insider trading theories developed in futures markets could plausibly be applied to event contracts.

Why this matters

This guidance signals that compliance infrastructure for prediction-market operators is no longer optional box-checking — it is a live legal exposure. Traders who hold information advantages on economic or political outcomes may face the same legal jeopardy as commodity futures insiders, a doctrine that has never been systematically applied to event contracts before. Prediction-market operators should watch for CFTC rulemaking or enforcement actions that use this legal theory as a template.
