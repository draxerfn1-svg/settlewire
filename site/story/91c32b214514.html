CFTC Proposes Framework to Define Permissible Prediction Market Contracts

Published JUN 10, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The CFTC under the Trump administration has put forward new rules aimed at clarifying which types of contracts are legally permitted on prediction markets.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission, led by a Trump-appointed regulator, has proposed a regulatory framework that would establish clearer boundaries around what event contracts prediction markets can legally offer, according to the Wall Street Journal. The move represents the most significant regulatory guidance for the sector in years.

The proposed rules would give platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket a more defined legal pathway for listing contracts, potentially opening the door to a broader range of political, economic, and event-based markets. According to the WSJ, the proposal comes as prediction markets have surged in public visibility following heavy trading activity around the 2024 U.S. election cycle.

The CFTC has historically used a case-by-case review process to evaluate event contracts, creating uncertainty for operators. A formal rule would replace that ad hoc approach with standardized criteria, which industry participants have long requested as a prerequisite for scaling their platforms and attracting institutional participation.

Why this matters

A codified CFTC framework could dramatically accelerate contract listings on regulated U.S. platforms, shifting volume from offshore venues like Polymarket back to domestic competitors. Traders should watch for which contract categories get explicitly blessed or banned, as that will reshape market liquidity and arbitrage opportunities. Operators and investors in Kalshi or ForecastEx stand to benefit most if the rules reduce compliance ambiguity.
