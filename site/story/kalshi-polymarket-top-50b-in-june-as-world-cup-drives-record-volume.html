Kalshi, Polymarket Top $50B in June as World Cup Drives Record Volume

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 11:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi, Polymarket, and Robinhood's Rothera joint venture collectively surpassed $50 billion in notional trading volume in June 2026, their biggest month ever, fueled by the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

Kalshi led the surge with $31 billion in total notional volume in June — a 70%-plus jump from May — with sports contracts comprising roughly 85% of activity, according to Dune Analytics data cited by CoinDesk. World Cup-specific contracts on Kalshi alone reached $22.42 billion. Polymarket's international exchange hit a monthly record of $10.8 billion, while its separate U.S.-regulated platform logged $3.5 billion, nearly twice May's total.

Rothera, the prediction-market venture launched jointly by Robinhood and Susquehanna International Group in June, processed $2 billion in its debut month and already commands 7% of the U.S. prediction market, per Bank of America estimates reported by CoinDesk. The platform's rapid early traction underscores how quickly new entrants can gain ground when major sporting events coincide with a product launch.

Kalshi told CoinDesk its World Cup growth was driven in part by a demographic rarely seen on traditional sportsbooks: female and first-time bettors. Separately, institutional trading desks at major financial firms are reportedly building dedicated positions in event contracts, treating them as financial derivatives rather than gambling products — a framing that could reshape regulatory and competitive dynamics.

Why this matters

The $50 billion figure gives prediction-market traders hard evidence that liquidity is now deep enough to support sophisticated strategies, narrowing spreads and improving price discovery on major events. For operators and new entrants, Rothera's 7% market-share grab in a single debut month signals that distribution partnerships — not just product — are the new competitive moat. Regulation watchers should note that institutional desk formation and demographic broadening strengthen the 'financial instrument' argument that platforms like Kalshi have made before the CFTC.
