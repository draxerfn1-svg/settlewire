LA Therapists Report Rise in Youth Gambling Tied to Kalshi

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 12:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Los Angeles-area therapists are reporting an increase in young patients presenting with gambling problems, and they are pointing to prediction-markets platform Kalshi as a contributing factor.

According to LAist, mental health practitioners in the Los Angeles area have begun seeing a notable uptick in younger clients struggling with compulsive wagering behavior, with several therapists specifically naming Kalshi — the CFTC-regulated prediction-markets exchange — as the platform most frequently mentioned by affected patients.

Kalshi, which gained federal regulatory approval to offer event contracts in the U.S., markets its products as financial instruments rather than gambling, a distinction that critics argue makes it more accessible and less stigmatized for younger users who might otherwise avoid traditional sportsbooks or casinos. The platform allows users to bet on outcomes ranging from economic indicators to political elections.

LAist's report does not include aggregate patient counts or clinical data, but the anecdotal pattern described by multiple therapists suggests that the mainstreaming of prediction markets — backed by CFTC legitimacy and app-based accessibility — may be creating behavioral-risk pathways that existing gambling-harm frameworks were not designed to address.

Why this matters

Regulatory approval from the CFTC gave Kalshi a 'not gambling' shield that may be blunting public-health warning systems designed to catch problem gamblers early. If harm data accumulates and reaches lawmakers, it could accelerate calls for CFTC to impose participation guardrails — age verification, exposure limits — that would directly reshape how event-contract platforms operate. Prediction-market traders should watch whether this narrative gains traction in California, a state with outsized influence on consumer-finance regulation.
