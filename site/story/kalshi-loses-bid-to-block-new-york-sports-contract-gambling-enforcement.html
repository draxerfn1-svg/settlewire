Kalshi loses bid to block New York sports-contract gambling enforcement

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 02:48 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Judge Analisa Torres on Monday denied Kalshi's emergency motion to prevent New York authorities from enforcing state gambling laws against its sports-event prediction contracts.

Judge Analisa Torres of the Southern District of New York ruled against Kalshi's request for a preliminary injunction that would have halted New York's enforcement action targeting the exchange's sports-event contracts. Kalshi had argued that its CFTC-regulated event contracts should be shielded from state gambling statutes, a position the court declined to accept at this stage, according to Reuters and The Block.

The dispute centers on whether Kalshi's federally licensed sports-event contracts — which allow traders to take positions on outcomes like game winners — constitute illegal gambling under New York law. New York officials have moved to restrict access to the contracts for state residents, setting up a direct conflict between state gambling regulators and a CFTC-designated contract market.

The ruling is a significant setback for Kalshi, which launched sports-event contracts in late 2024 after winning a landmark federal appellate decision against the CFTC. The company had positioned federal preemption as a core defense against state-level challenges, and Torres's denial keeps the New York enforcement action live while litigation continues.

Why this matters

This ruling signals that federal CFTC authorization may not automatically preempt state gambling enforcement, creating a patchwork-regulation risk that could fragment prediction-market liquidity by state. Traders in New York-restricted markets face potential access cutoffs, directly shrinking the pool of participants pricing sports contracts. Regulation watchers should note this sets a template other states could follow to challenge CFTC-licensed event contracts without waiting for federal legislative clarity.
