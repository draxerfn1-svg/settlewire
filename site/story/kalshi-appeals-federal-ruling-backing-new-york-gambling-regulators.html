Kalshi appeals federal ruling backing New York gambling regulators

Published JUL 09, 2026 · 16:05 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi has filed an appeal after a federal judge ruled in favor of New York gambling regulators who sought to restrict the prediction-market platform's sports event contracts in the state.

According to Gothamist, Kalshi is challenging a federal court decision that sided with New York gambling regulators attempting to block the company's sports-related event contracts. The appeal escalates a legal battle that has become a flashpoint for how prediction markets are classified under state versus federal jurisdiction.

At the core of the dispute is whether the Commodity Futures Trading Commission's federal oversight of Kalshi's event contracts preempts state-level gambling laws. New York regulators have argued that Kalshi's sports contracts constitute illegal gambling under state law, a position the federal district court appeared to validate in its ruling.

The case carries broad implications for the prediction-market industry, which has been rapidly expanding into sports event contracts since the CFTC declined to block Kalshi's offerings. A ruling against Kalshi at the appellate level could empower other states to impose similar restrictions, potentially fragmenting the market across state lines.

Why this matters

An adverse appellate outcome could set precedent allowing any state to override federal CFTC authorization, fundamentally threatening the national scalability of regulated prediction markets. Traders on sports event contracts should watch this case closely, as a ruling against Kalshi could reduce liquidity or restrict participation by geography. Operators and investors will be pricing in regulatory risk across the sector until a final decision is reached.
