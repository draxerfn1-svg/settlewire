Large Polymarket Wagers Placed on Putin Losing Power

Published JUL 02, 2026 · 00:40 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Significant sums have been wagered on Polymarket contracts tied to Russian President Vladimir Putin's political fate, drawing widespread attention.

According to NBC News, notable bets have surfaced on Polymarket — the crypto-based prediction market platform — centered on whether Vladimir Putin will remain in power or face some form of political downfall. The contracts have attracted attention both for their size and for the geopolitical signal they may carry.

Polymarket has increasingly become a venue for high-stakes political wagering beyond U.S. elections, with users placing large individual positions on foreign-leader outcomes. The Putin contracts reflect a broader trend of traders using the platform to speculate on low-probability but high-impact geopolitical events, where contract payouts can be substantial if rare scenarios materialize.

This follows Polymarket's surge in mainstream visibility after the 2024 U.S. presidential election cycle, during which the platform recorded hundreds of millions in trading volume. Political contracts involving foreign heads of state now represent a growing segment, raising questions about the platform's data value as a geopolitical forecasting tool.

Why this matters

Large, visible bets on sovereign-leader contracts can move implied probabilities significantly, making these markets both a signal and a target for informed or well-capitalized traders. Regulators and competing platforms will watch whether such contracts attract scrutiny under event-contract rules. For operators, high-profile geopolitical markets drive user acquisition but also increase reputational and compliance risk.
