South Korea grants Polymarket hearing on illegal gambling charges

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 09:40 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

South Korean authorities have given Polymarket an opportunity to formally respond to allegations that its platform constitutes illegal gambling under local law, according to SBC News.

South Korean regulators have extended Polymarket the right to respond to illegal gambling allegations leveled against the prediction-markets platform, according to SBC News. The move signals that authorities are following a due-process procedure before rendering any formal ruling on whether Polymarket's event contracts fall under the country's gambling statutes.

South Korea has historically maintained strict prohibitions on unlicensed gambling activity, and regulators have increasingly scrutinized offshore prediction-market and crypto-adjacent platforms operating within its borders. The decision to allow Polymarket a response period suggests the case has not yet reached a definitive enforcement stage, leaving open the possibility of a negotiated outcome or regulatory exemption.

Polymarket, which surged in global prominence during the 2024 U.S. election cycle, has faced a patchwork of regulatory challenges across multiple jurisdictions. The company was previously barred from serving U.S. customers following a 2022 CFTC settlement, and the South Korean probe adds another front to its ongoing international compliance battles.

Why this matters

A South Korean ruling against Polymarket could set a precedent that other Asia-Pacific regulators follow, compressing the platform's addressable market at a critical growth phase. Prediction-market traders should watch whether Korea classifies event contracts as gambling or financial instruments — that distinction will ripple into liquidity and user-base projections. Operators eyeing Asian expansion face a live test case that may define jurisdictional risk pricing for the entire sector.
