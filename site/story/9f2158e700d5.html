Kalshi June Volume Hits $9.4B Record as World Cup Drives Surge

Published JUL 04, 2026 · 16:50 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi posted its highest-ever monthly trading volume in June at nearly $9.4 billion, with the expanded 48-team FIFA World Cup serving as the primary catalyst.

According to Cointelegraph, citing DefiLlama data, Kalshi's June volume of roughly $9.4 billion represented a dramatic jump from approximately $5.3 billion in May. Polymarket International also gained ground, rising from around $3.5 billion in May to approximately $4.3 billion in June. CNBC reported the World Cup became the single biggest driver of prediction market trading activity during the month.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup, which began June 11, is the first edition to feature 48 national teams rather than 32, creating a larger slate of matchups for traders to wager on. Knockout-round contests have drawn the heaviest activity: Canada's Round of 16 clash with Morocco alone generated over $48 million on Kalshi and more than $26.8 million on Polymarket, per Cointelegraph. The US team's advancement market pulled in over $2.1 million on Kalshi.

The record volumes arrive amid mounting legal pressure, according to Cointelegraph, with nearly a dozen US states having moved against prediction market operators by March, some attempting to classify the platforms under existing gambling frameworks or state tax regimes. The regulatory tension underscores how rapidly the sector's commercial scale is outpacing its legal clarity.

Why this matters

Record volumes strengthen Kalshi's and Polymarket's negotiating positions with regulators, making outright bans politically harder as user bases and tax-revenue arguments grow. Traders should watch whether knockout-round liquidity depth translates into tighter spreads on future sports contracts. State-level legal actions could fragment market access geographically, creating arbitrage opportunities or dead zones depending on jurisdiction.
