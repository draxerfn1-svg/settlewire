CFTC Draft Rules Would Bar Event Contracts on Terror, Assassinations

Published JUN 10, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Federal regulators have released proposed rules for prediction markets that would explicitly prohibit trading on terrorism events and political assassinations.

The CFTC has put forward a framework governing event contracts that draws hard lines around certain categories of activity, according to CNBC. Among the explicitly banned subjects are terrorism and assassinations, which regulators have flagged as contrary to the public interest regardless of any potential price-discovery value.

The proposed rules arrive as prediction markets have surged in mainstream visibility, particularly following high-profile election trading on platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket. Regulators appear to be trying to establish guardrails that allow legitimate forecasting activity to continue while blocking contracts that could create harmful incentives or be seen as profiting from violence.

The CFTC's move represents one of the most substantive regulatory actions targeting the event-contract space in years. The proposal is expected to enter a public comment period, giving platforms, traders, and legal advocates an opportunity to shape the final language before any rules take effect.

Why this matters

For prediction-market operators like Kalshi and ForecastEx, the rulemaking creates both clarity and constraint — clearer legal footing for permissible contracts, but potential compliance costs for policing listed markets. Traders should watch whether the final rules define banned categories narrowly or broadly, since vague language around 'terrorism' could chill legitimate geopolitical contracts. Regulation watchers will note this sets a precedent for future CFTC intervention in what topics event-contract markets may touch.
