Michigan court halts Kalshi sports event contracts

Published JUL 06, 2026 · 13:24 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

A Michigan court issued a ruling sidelining Kalshi's sports prediction markets, delivering a significant legal blow to the federally regulated exchange's expansion into sports event contracts.

A Michigan court moved to block Kalshi from operating sports-related prediction markets in the state, according to Axios, marking one of the first successful state-level legal challenges against the New York-based CFTC-regulated exchange. The ruling raises immediate questions about whether other states could mount similar actions against federally authorized event-contract markets.

Kalshi has positioned its sports markets as legal under federal jurisdiction via its CFTC designation as a designated contract market (DCM), a framework the exchange argues preempts state gambling laws. The Michigan ruling challenges that preemption argument directly, creating a fault line between federal event-contract regulation and state-level gaming authority that courts have not previously resolved at this scale.

The decision could reverberate across the prediction-market industry, which includes competitors such as Polymarket and ForecastEx, all of which are navigating the unresolved tension between federal financial regulation and state gaming statutes. Kalshi had aggressively expanded into sports contracts following a separate federal court victory in 2024 that cleared its political event markets.

Why this matters

This ruling is a critical data point for traders pricing the long-term viability of Kalshi's sports-market volume, since a patchwork of state injunctions could materially fragment liquidity. For regulation watchers, it tests whether CFTC designation genuinely preempts state gambling law — a question with industry-wide consequences. If the Michigan precedent spreads, operators may face a compliance map as complex as sports-betting licensing, sharply raising barriers to entry.
