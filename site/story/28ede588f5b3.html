DOJ and CFTC file landmark insider trading charges tied to prediction markets

Published APR 27, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Federal prosecutors and the CFTC jointly brought the first criminal and civil insider trading case targeting manipulation of prediction market contracts.

Federal prosecutors and the CFTC jointly brought the first criminal and civil insider trading case targeting manipulation of prediction market contracts.

Why this matters

This unprecedented enforcement action signals that regulators now view prediction market contracts as genuine financial instruments subject to the same insider trading rules as securities, which could force platforms to build surveillance and compliance infrastructure similar to traditional exchanges. Operators and market makers should expect heightened scrutiny of information advantages, while the case may accelerate CFTC rulemaking that formally defines what constitutes material nonpublic information in event contract markets.
