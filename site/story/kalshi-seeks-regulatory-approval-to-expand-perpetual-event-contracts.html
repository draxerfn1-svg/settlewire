Kalshi seeks regulatory approval to expand perpetual event contracts

Published JUL 09, 2026 · 21:32 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Prediction market operator Kalshi is in active discussions with U.S. regulators to extend its never-expiring derivatives products into new market categories, Reuters reported exclusively.

Kalshi, the CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange, is negotiating with regulators to broaden the scope of its perpetual event contracts — instruments that carry no fixed expiration date — beyond their current coverage, according to Reuters. The company has been pushing the regulatory envelope since winning a landmark legal battle allowing it to offer election markets.

Perpetual event contracts represent a structural innovation in the prediction-market space, borrowing a mechanism popularized by crypto derivatives platforms but applying it to real-world event outcomes. Expanding these instruments into new subject areas would require CFTC sign-off, given that all Kalshi products must be certified or approved as designated contract market offerings.

The Reuters report did not specify which new categories Kalshi is targeting, but the company has previously explored markets in economics, weather, and entertainment. Any regulatory green light would likely intensify competitive pressure on rivals such as Polymarket, which operates offshore and is not subject to the same U.S. approval process.

Why this matters

A successful regulatory expansion of perpetual contracts could dramatically increase Kalshi's addressable market, giving traders longer-horizon positions without rollover costs and drawing liquidity away from offshore venues. For CFTC watchers, how regulators respond signals how far the agency is willing to stretch event-contract definitions under its current leadership. Competing platforms and their market-makers should price in the possibility of a significantly larger, better-capitalized regulated rival within the next 12 months.
