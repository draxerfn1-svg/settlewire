SEC and CFTC jointly seek public input on event contract regulation

Published JUN 29, 2026 · 17:50 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The SEC and CFTC have issued a joint request for public comment aimed at clarifying the regulatory boundary between securities and event contracts.

According to JD Supra, the SEC and CFTC are jointly soliciting input from market participants and the public to help draw clearer jurisdictional lines around event contracts and other novel derivatives products. The move signals that both agencies recognize the existing regulatory framework may be ill-suited to handle a new generation of financial instruments.

Event contracts — wagers on real-world outcomes traded on platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket — have long existed in a gray zone between commodity futures regulation under the CFTC and securities oversight by the SEC. The joint inquiry suggests regulators are finally willing to formalize which agency governs which product type, a question that has generated years of legal uncertainty.

The request for comment is a procedural step that typically precedes rulemaking or formal guidance. Industry participants, including prediction market operators and their legal counsel, will have an opportunity to shape the eventual framework, according to JD Supra, making this a rare opening for the sector to influence its own regulatory future.

Why this matters

A clearer jurisdictional split between the SEC and CFTC would reduce compliance costs and litigation risk for prediction market operators, potentially accelerating product launches. Traders should watch for any signal that event contracts tied to securities-like outcomes — such as stock price events — could shift to SEC oversight, which carries stricter rules. The outcome of this process could also set precedent for how crypto-based prediction markets are classified.
