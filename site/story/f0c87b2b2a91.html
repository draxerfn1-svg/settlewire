Polymarket Whale Drops $400K on Putin Losing Power by 2026

Published JUL 02, 2026 · 13:29 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

An anonymous Polymarket user has placed a $400,000 wager predicting Russian President Vladimir Putin will lose power before the end of 2026.

A mystery bettor on Polymarket has staked $400,000 on a market asking whether Vladimir Putin will no longer hold power in Russia by the close of 2026, according to Forbes. The bet is one of the largest single political wagers seen on the decentralized prediction platform, drawing significant attention to the contract.

The identity of the trader remains unknown, consistent with Polymarket's pseudonymous, crypto-wallet-based structure. Forbes reported the bet pushes meaningful liquidity into what had been a lower-volume geopolitical market, potentially shifting the contract's implied probability and attracting counter-bettors who believe Putin's grip on power will hold through the deadline.

Large single-position wagers on geopolitical markets have become a recurring feature on Polymarket, following high-profile election bets during the 2024 U.S. presidential cycle. These outsized positions raise ongoing questions about informed trading, market manipulation, and whether concentrated liquidity accurately reflects crowd-sourced probability or individual conviction.

Why this matters

A $400,000 position in a single geopolitical contract tests Polymarket's liquidity depth and price discovery integrity — traders on the other side must assess whether this whale has genuine intelligence or is simply making a speculative play. For regulators, outsized anonymous bets on foreign-leader removal markets invite scrutiny around market manipulation thresholds. Platform operators should watch whether the odds shift materially, signaling herd-following behavior that could distort the market's informational value.
