Kalshi, Polymarket Face Core Legal Question: Gambling or Finance?

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 09:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Bloomberg Law News examines the central regulatory debate over whether US prediction markets like Kalshi and Polymarket constitute legal financial instruments or illegal gambling under American law.

The classification question has profound consequences for the industry: if event contracts are deemed financial products, they fall under CFTC oversight and enjoy federal preemption of state gambling laws — the legal shield Kalshi successfully invoked in its 2024 court victory over the CFTC's own attempt to block election contracts. If classified as gambling, platforms risk prosecution under state statutes and the federal Wire Act.

Kalshi, which operates as a CFTC-designated contract market, and Polymarket, which serves US users through a decentralized model while facing its own regulatory scrutiny, represent the two dominant poles of the industry. Their differing structures illustrate why regulators and courts have struggled to apply existing legal frameworks consistently to prediction market products.

The distinction hinges on factors including whether contracts are cash-settled, exchange-traded, and tied to measurable economic events — criteria the CFTC uses to evaluate event contracts under the Commodity Exchange Act. According to Bloomberg Law News, the ongoing legal ambiguity continues to shape how platforms design products, restrict user access, and lobby for clearer statutory guidance from Congress.

Why this matters

Traders on prediction markets face direct platform-access and contract-availability risk depending on how courts and regulators ultimately resolve this classification battle. A ruling favoring the gambling label in any major jurisdiction could trigger immediate withdrawal of products and user funds, repricing risk across open contracts. Operators and investors watching Kalshi's potential IPO trajectory should treat this legal uncertainty as a material variable in any valuation model.
