Tribal gaming leader warns prediction markets threaten sovereign casino revenues

Published JUL 12, 2026 · 11:10 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

James Siva, a tribal gaming advocate, warned in a Tribal Business News Q&A that the rapid expansion of regulated prediction markets poses a direct competitive threat to Native American tribal casino operations.

According to Tribal Business News, Siva outlined how federally regulated prediction markets — platforms like Kalshi that now offer contracts on sports outcomes and other events — are drawing wagering dollars that historically flowed to tribal gaming facilities. Tribal casinos generated roughly $41 billion in revenue in 2023, a base Siva argues is now under new pressure.

Siva's concern centers on regulatory asymmetry: tribal gaming operates under the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act and negotiated state compacts, a framework that imposes compliance costs and geographic restrictions, while CFTC-regulated event contracts face a lighter and nationally uniform ruleset. That gap, he argued, gives prediction-market platforms a structural advantage in capturing mobile, at-home bettors.

The interview arrives as the CFTC has allowed Kalshi's congressional-election contracts and sports-event contracts to proceed, setting precedents that tribal leaders fear will be extended broadly. Siva called on tribal governments to engage federal regulators directly before the market-design window closes, signaling that organized lobbying efforts may be forthcoming.

Why this matters

Tribal opposition represents a politically potent new constituency in the CFTC's regulatory environment, and organized pushback from sovereign nations could slow or complicate future event-contract approvals. Prediction-market operators expanding into sports and entertainment verticals should price in the risk of new legislative challenges backed by tribal gaming's substantial lobbying infrastructure. For traders, any regulatory slowdown in contract-category expansion would directly constrain the universe of markets available to trade.
