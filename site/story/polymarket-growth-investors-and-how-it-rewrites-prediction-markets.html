Polymarket Growth, Investors, and How It Rewrites Prediction Markets

Published JUL 01, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket, the crypto-based prediction market platform founded in 2020, has expanded into one of the largest event-contract venues globally, attracting major investors and reshaping how users forecast real-world outcomes.

According to Encyclopedia Britannica, Polymarket was founded in 2020 and quickly grew into a dominant force in prediction markets, allowing users to trade event contracts on outcomes ranging from elections to scientific discoveries. The platform operates on the Polygon blockchain, enabling low-fee transactions that made participation accessible to a broad retail audience.

FinTech Magazine notes that Polymarket's model differs from traditional prediction platforms by leaning heavily into cryptocurrency infrastructure, drawing liquidity from decentralized-finance users alongside forecasting enthusiasts. The platform reportedly processed billions of dollars in trading volume during the 2024 U.S. presidential election cycle, cementing its profile as a mainstream forecasting tool.

Polymarket has attracted backing from prominent venture investors and has navigated a complex U.S. regulatory environment — the CFTC settled with the company in 2022 over unregistered binary-options offerings. Despite that, the platform continues operating internationally while U.S. users face access restrictions, a tension that defines its current growth phase.

Why this matters

Polymarket's scale makes it the de-facto benchmark for prediction-market liquidity, meaning any regulatory shift targeting it would reprice risk across the entire sector. Traders on competing platforms like Kalshi should watch Polymarket volume as a leading indicator of event-contract market sentiment. Its ongoing U.S. access restrictions also keep CFTC event-contract rulemaking squarely in focus for operators seeking domestic legitimacy.
