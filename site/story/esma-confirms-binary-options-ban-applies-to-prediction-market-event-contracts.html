ESMA confirms binary options ban applies to prediction market event contracts

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 12:49 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Europe's top securities regulator ESMA has ruled that its existing product intervention measures banning binary options extend to event contracts traded on prediction markets, according to fintechanddigitalassets.com.

ESMA's confirmation closes a regulatory ambiguity that prediction market operators in the EU had been navigating since binary options were banned for retail clients under product intervention powers first enacted in 2018. The ruling explicitly maps event contracts — the core instrument of platforms like Polymarket — to the binary options definition, according to fintechanddigitalassets.com.

The intervention measures, which have been periodically renewed by ESMA and national competent authorities, prohibit the marketing, distribution, and sale of binary options to retail investors. By confirming that event contracts fall within scope, ESMA signals that any EU-facing prediction market offering yes/no outcome contracts must comply with those restrictions or face enforcement action.

The ruling creates a significant barrier for US-based platforms eyeing European expansion and raises immediate compliance questions for any operator currently serving EU retail users. National regulators in member states now have a clearer legal basis to act against non-compliant platforms, potentially mirroring the kind of regulatory pressure Polymarket faced from US authorities.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders, this effectively shuts the door on compliant retail access to event contracts across the EU's 27 member states, concentrating legal market activity outside Europe. Platform operators must now either restrict EU users entirely or pursue a regulated derivatives exemption — an expensive and uncertain path. Regulation watchers should note this sets a precedent that could influence how other jurisdictions, including the UK's FCA, classify event contracts going forward.
