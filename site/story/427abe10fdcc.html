U.S. Users Moved $571M on Polymarket Despite American Ban

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

American traders collectively wagered $571 million on Polymarket's political event contracts even though the platform is prohibited from serving U.S. customers.

According to CoinDesk, U.S.-based users managed to access and trade on Polymarket to the tune of $571 million in political prediction markets, circumventing the platform's formal ban on American participation. Polymarket has been officially off-limits to U.S. residents since the company settled with the CFTC in 2022, agreeing to block domestic users as part of that resolution.

The scale of the figure underscores how ineffective geographic restrictions can be when a platform operates on public blockchain infrastructure. Users relying on VPNs or other tools to mask their location can sidestep IP-based controls with relative ease, and on-chain activity remains visible and traceable regardless of those workarounds, per CoinDesk's reporting.

Polymarket saw explosive growth during the 2024 U.S. election cycle, becoming one of the most-watched forecasting venues globally. The revelation that a substantial share of that volume originated domestically raises pointed questions about the enforceability of existing CFTC consent orders and the regulatory framework governing prediction-market access more broadly.

Why this matters

For prediction-market operators, this story signals that CFTC enforcement of user-ban provisions remains a live risk — a fresh complaint or contempt action against Polymarket could set precedents affecting Kalshi and ForecastEx's own compliance obligations. Regulators now have hard volume data that makes inaction harder to justify publicly. Traders who used VPNs to access Polymarket may also face personal legal exposure if authorities decide to pursue individual cases.
