Polymarket enables instant Bitcoin deposits via Lightning Network

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 19:49 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket activated instant Bitcoin deposits through the Lightning Network on Tuesday, powered by the Spark protocol, giving users a faster, lower-cost on-ramp to fund prediction-market positions.

Polymarket integrated the Spark protocol to support Lightning Network deposits, allowing bettors to fund accounts with Bitcoin almost instantly rather than waiting for on-chain confirmations. According to Bitcoin Magazine, the move marks a notable infrastructure upgrade for the prediction market platform, which has historically relied on USDC on Polygon for most user transactions.

Lightning Network payments settle in seconds and carry fees measured in fractions of a cent, a stark contrast to the variable gas costs and multi-minute waits associated with base-layer Bitcoin transactions. Spark acts as the bridging layer that converts incoming Lightning payments into a form Polymarket's existing smart-contract architecture can recognize and process.

The addition broadens Polymarket's potential user base to the large segment of crypto holders whose primary liquid asset is Bitcoin rather than stablecoins. It also positions the platform competitively as rivals such as Kalshi expand their own payment rails in a market environment where frictionless deposits are increasingly viewed as a key retention metric.

Why this matters

Faster, cheaper deposits lower the activation barrier for casual bettors, which historically correlates with higher open-interest volume and tighter spreads on Polymarket contracts. For prediction-market operators watching the space, Lightning integration signals that the sector is maturing beyond stablecoin-only infrastructure toward multi-asset liquidity. Regulators tracking event contracts may also scrutinize whether pseudonymous Lightning deposits complicate KYC obligations already under debate at the CFTC.
