Michigan Daily: Prediction Markets Deserve Recognition as Public Good

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 20:54 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

The Michigan Daily published an opinion piece arguing that prediction markets generate genuine social value beyond speculation.

The Michigan Daily ran an opinion column making the case that prediction markets serve a broader societal function, positioning them not merely as gambling instruments but as tools for aggregating dispersed information into actionable forecasts. The piece comes amid a period of heightened mainstream attention to platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket.

According to The Michigan Daily, the information-discovery function of prediction markets can benefit policymakers, researchers, and the public by surfacing crowd wisdom on outcomes ranging from elections to economic indicators. The argument mirrors longstanding academic defenses of market-based forecasting that predate the current retail boom.

The opinion lands as the CFTC continues to weigh the regulatory boundaries of event contracts in the United States, and as platforms like Kalshi push for expanded contract offerings. Student and campus media engaging with the topic signals growing mainstream normalization of prediction markets as a legitimate financial and epistemic instrument.

Why this matters

Campus opinion pieces rarely move markets, but this one reflects a broader normalization trend that matters for regulatory sentiment — regulators and legislators are sensitive to public perception of prediction markets as gambling versus public goods. A sustained positive framing in mainstream and academic media could ease political resistance to CFTC approval of new contract categories. Operators tracking legitimacy risk should note the shift in narrative outside financial media.
