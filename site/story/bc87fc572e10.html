Michigan's Legal Fight Against Kalshi Reaches New Turning Point

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 23:10 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Michigan's ongoing regulatory dispute with prediction-market operator Kalshi has entered a new phase, continuing a clash over whether the platform's event contracts fall under state sports-betting law.

According to WZZM13.com, Michigan's legal confrontation with Kalshi has taken another significant turn, extending a dispute that centers on the state's attempt to classify Kalshi's sports-related event contracts as regulated sports betting subject to state oversight.

Kalshi, which holds federal authorization from the CFTC to offer event contracts, has argued that federal jurisdiction preempts state-level regulation — a position it has successfully advanced in other venues. Michigan regulators, however, contend that contracts tied to sports outcomes fall squarely within the state's gaming authority.

The case is part of a broader national pattern in which state gaming regulators have moved to assert authority over CFTC-regulated prediction markets, even as platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket expand their sports-event contract offerings to retail users across the country.

Why this matters

This case is a bellwether for how aggressively states will contest CFTC preemption arguments — a legal theory that underpins Kalshi's entire state-by-state expansion strategy. A ruling against Kalshi in Michigan could embolden other states to pursue similar actions, fragmenting the market. Conversely, a federal preemption win would clear a significant legal runway for event-contract platforms nationwide.
