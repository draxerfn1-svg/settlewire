Polymarket advances U.S. return with new margin trading framework

Published JUL 10, 2026 · 09:37 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket is moving closer to a formal U.S. relaunch by developing a margin trading plan, marking the prediction market platform's most concrete step yet toward re-entering the American market.

Polymarket, which blocked U.S. users following a 2022 CFTC settlement that included a $1.4 million fine, is now pursuing a margin trading structure as part of its strategy to legally re-enter the American market, according to CoinDesk. The move signals the platform is actively designing a product architecture that could satisfy domestic regulatory requirements rather than simply waiting for a friendlier enforcement environment.

The margin trading plan represents a meaningful product pivot for Polymarket, which has operated primarily as a straightforward binary-outcome contract platform. Introducing margin mechanics would bring it closer to the futures-style event contracts that regulated U.S. venues like Kalshi and ForecastEx already offer, potentially giving Polymarket a viable compliance pathway under CFTC oversight.

The timing is notable: the CFTC under the current administration has signaled a more accommodating posture toward innovative financial products, and Kalshi's court victories over the agency have already reshaped the regulatory landscape for event contracts. A margin-enabled Polymarket could compete directly with those registered designated contract markets if it secures the necessary approvals.

Why this matters

A Polymarket U.S. relaunch would immediately intensify competition with Kalshi and ForecastEx, pressuring all platforms on liquidity, contract variety, and fee structures. Prediction-market traders should watch whether Polymarket pursues full CFTC registration or a lighter-touch structure, as that choice will define which contracts it can legally offer Americans. Regulators and compliance watchers will use this filing as a stress test for how the agency applies its event-contract framework to crypto-native platforms seeking re-entry.
