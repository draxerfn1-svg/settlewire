Willkie Farr & Gallagher Publishes Prediction Market Exchange Regulatory Overview

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 16:36 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Law firm Willkie Farr & Gallagher released a "State of Play" briefing on prediction market exchanges, summarizing the current regulatory and competitive landscape for event-contract platforms.

Willkie Farr & Gallagher, a prominent law firm with deep CFTC-adjacent practice, published a structured overview titled 'State of Play — Prediction Market Exchanges,' signaling growing institutional legal interest in the sector. The document addresses the framework governing designated contract markets (DCMs) that offer event contracts, including platforms such as Kalshi and ForecastEx.

The briefing comes as prediction markets have gained mainstream attention following high-profile election contract trading in 2024, which drew record volume on Kalshi and Polymarket. Regulatory scrutiny has intensified in parallel, with the CFTC actively revisiting its event-contract rules and self-certification processes used by exchanges to list new markets.

According to Willkie Farr & Gallagher, the 'state of play' framing implies a fluid and actively contested legal environment — one where exchange applicants, incumbents, and regulators are still negotiating the boundaries of permissible contracts. The publication of such a memo by a major firm typically signals client demand from financial institutions exploring entry into the space.

Why this matters

A Biglaw firm codifying the prediction-market regulatory landscape suggests institutional capital is seriously evaluating entry, which could accelerate competition against Kalshi and Polymarket. Traders should watch for new DCM applicants as a leading indicator of market expansion and potential liquidity shifts. Regulatory clarity memos of this type can also precede formal CFTC rulemaking comments, making the next comment period a key event to monitor.
