Spotify Demands Kalshi and Polymarket Drop Music Betting Markets

Published JUL 03, 2026 · 11:57 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Spotify has formally asked prediction market platforms Kalshi and Polymarket to remove its branding after manipulators gamed stream counts to influence music-related contract settlements.

Spotify contacted both Kalshi and Polymarket requesting they strip the streaming giant's branding from markets tied to music performance metrics, according to Bloomberg via The Block. The move follows discoveries that bad actors artificially inflated stream counts on the platform specifically to influence how those event contracts settled.

The incident highlights a core vulnerability in prediction markets that rely on third-party data sources: any measurable metric that can be gamed becomes a potential attack vector once money is riding on its outcome. Music streaming numbers, unlike election results or asset prices, are relatively easy to manipulate through bot activity or coordinated listening campaigns.

Both Kalshi and Polymarket have expanded aggressively into entertainment and pop-culture markets over the past year, seeking to broaden audiences beyond politics and finance. This episode may force platforms to rethink how they source, verify, and weight data for non-traditional event contracts, or risk similar manipulation attempts across other easily gamed metrics.

Why this matters

For prediction-market operators, this case sets a precedent that brand owners can push back against markets using their platforms as data oracles, creating legal and reputational exposure. Traders in entertainment markets should now price in elevated settlement risk whenever the underlying metric is a manipulable third-party figure. Regulators watching event-contract design will likely cite this as evidence that oracle integrity standards need formal codification.
