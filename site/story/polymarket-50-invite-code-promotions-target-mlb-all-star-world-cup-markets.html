Polymarket $50 Invite-Code Promotions Target MLB All-Star, World Cup Markets

Published JUL 14, 2026 · 13:45 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket is offering $50 bonuses via invite codes including "CUSE" and "SBWIRE" to new users in New York, Pennsylvania, and Texas ahead of the MLB All-Star Game and World Cup Semifinal on July 14.

Polymarket is running geo-targeted bonus promotions in at least three U.S. states — New York, Pennsylvania, and Texas — timed to two high-profile July events: the MLB All-Star Game and the FIFA World Cup Semifinal. New users who enter codes such as "CUSE" or "SBWIRE" at sign-up receive a $50 credit, according to coverage from Syracuse.com and Golfweek.

The promotions appear coordinated around July 14, the date of both marquee events, suggesting Polymarket is deliberately stacking its marketing calendar around peak sports-news moments. Invite-code campaigns of this kind are a standard customer-acquisition tool in prediction markets, designed to convert casual sports fans into active traders on outcome contracts.

Polymarket has been expanding its U.S. user base aggressively following regulatory shifts that have given event-contract platforms more room to operate domestically. Targeting specific states with localized codes indicates the platform is running segmented campaigns, possibly calibrated to regional sports fanbases or differing state-level promotional rules.

Why this matters

Invite-code acquisition pushes ahead of major sporting events signal that Polymarket is betting heavily on sports-driven onboarding as a growth vector, which could move liquidity and open interest data that traders track as sentiment indicators. Rival platforms like Kalshi will face pressure to match promotional spending, raising customer-acquisition costs across the sector. Regulators watching event-contract marketing practices in restricted states should note the explicit state-level targeting embedded in these campaigns.
