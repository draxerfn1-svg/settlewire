Bernstein Flags Kalshi and Polymarket as Acquisition Targets

Published JUN 29, 2026 · 17:53 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Bernstein analysts say the two leading prediction market platforms could attract takeover bids as the sector gains mainstream legitimacy.

Wall Street research firm Bernstein has identified Kalshi and Polymarket as potential acquisition targets, according to Yahoo Finance. The note suggests that growing regulatory clarity and surging user interest in event contracts have made both platforms attractive to larger financial or media players looking to enter the space.

Kalshi, the only CFTC-regulated prediction market in the United States, and Polymarket, which operates via crypto rails and draws heavy international volume, have emerged as the two dominant players in the sector. Bernstein's coverage signals that institutional investors are beginning to view prediction markets as a credible asset class rather than a niche curiosity.

The analyst note arrives as prediction markets are experiencing unprecedented visibility following high-profile election market activity. Combined trading volumes across both platforms reached billions of dollars during the 2024 U.S. election cycle, demonstrating liquidity depth that could appeal to established exchanges, media companies, or financial data providers eyeing M&A.

Why this matters

A takeover of either platform would reshape the competitive landscape overnight, potentially giving a deep-pocketed acquirer regulatory leverage or distribution advantages that smaller rivals cannot match. For traders, consolidation could mean tighter spreads and better liquidity but also risks of market concentration. Regulation watchers should note that any acquisition of CFTC-regulated Kalshi would require close scrutiny of the buyer's fitness under existing event-contract rules.
