Prospect Markets enters US prediction markets via Crypto.com partnership

Published JUL 07, 2026 · 07:01 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Prospect Markets announced a Crypto.com-backed partnership to launch its prediction markets platform in the United States, expanding the competitive landscape for regulated event contracts.

Prospect Markets is entering the US prediction markets space through a strategic partnership supported by Crypto.com, according to Yahoo Finance. The move signals an intent to compete directly with established players like Kalshi and Polymarket in the regulated event-contract arena, which has seen surging interest following the 2024 US election cycle.

Crypto.com's backing provides Prospect Markets with both financial support and a built-in user base from the broader crypto ecosystem. The partnership structure suggests Prospect Markets will leverage Crypto.com's existing infrastructure and customer network to accelerate US market penetration, though specific contract volumes and launch timelines were not detailed in available reporting.

The US prediction markets sector has grown rapidly, with Kalshi and Polymarket collectively handling hundreds of millions of dollars in contract volume. New entrants face significant regulatory hurdles, as the CFTC maintains oversight of designated contract markets, meaning Prospect Markets will need to navigate licensing requirements before fully operating in the space.

Why this matters

A Crypto.com-backed entrant increases competitive pressure on Kalshi and Polymarket, which could compress margins and force product differentiation — a dynamic traders should watch when assessing platform liquidity depth. Regulatory watchers should note whether Prospect Markets pursues a CFTC designated contract market license or seeks an alternative structure, as that choice will define its legal risk profile. For prediction-market operators, this partnership model — pairing a niche event-contract platform with a major crypto exchange — may signal a replicable playbook that reshapes how new entrants capitalize efficiently.
