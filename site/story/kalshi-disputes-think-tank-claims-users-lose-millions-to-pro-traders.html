Kalshi disputes think tank claims users lose millions to pro traders

Published JUL 12, 2026 · 09:40 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Kalshi is publicly contesting allegations from a think tank asserting that retail users suffer significant losses when trading against professional market makers on the prediction platform.

According to Business Insider, Kalshi has entered into a public dispute with a think tank over research claiming that ordinary users routinely lose millions of dollars when matched against sophisticated, professional traders on the platform. The exchange pushed back against the findings, challenging the methodology and conclusions drawn about retail trader outcomes.

The core of the disagreement centers on whether Kalshi's market structure systematically disadvantages everyday participants relative to professional counterparties — a concern that mirrors longstanding debates in traditional financial markets about order flow and market maker advantages. The think tank's claims, if accepted broadly, could fuel regulatory scrutiny of event-contract exchanges.

Kalshi secured federal court victories allowing it to offer political event contracts in the U.S., making it one of the most prominent CFTC-regulated prediction market operators. Any credible narrative about retail harm on the platform arrives at a sensitive moment, as the broader industry is still establishing its legitimacy with regulators and the public.

Why this matters

If the think tank's retail-loss findings gain traction, they could give the CFTC or Congress fresh ammunition to tighten oversight of event-contract platforms beyond existing rules. Prediction-market operators beyond Kalshi — including Polymarket and ForecastEx — would face collateral reputational risk if the 'pros beat retail' story becomes the dominant media frame. Traders monitoring Kalshi's market volumes should watch for any chilling effect on retail participation as this dispute plays out publicly.
