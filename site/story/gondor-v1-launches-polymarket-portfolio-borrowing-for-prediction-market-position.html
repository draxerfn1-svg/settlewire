Gondor v1 launches Polymarket portfolio borrowing for prediction-market positions

Published JUL 13, 2026 · 19:37 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Gondor v1, a new DeFi protocol, will allow Polymarket users to use their entire prediction-market portfolio as collateral for loans, unlocking liquidity without forcing position exits.

Gondor v1 is set to introduce a borrowing mechanism that treats a Polymarket user's full portfolio of event-contract positions as eligible collateral, according to The Block. This marks a significant departure from typical DeFi lending, where borrowing is secured against spot tokens or LP positions rather than outcome shares on a prediction market.

The protocol targets active Polymarket traders who may hold significant unrealized value in open positions but lack access to liquid capital while waiting for event resolution. By collateralizing those positions, users could deploy borrowed funds elsewhere without closing their market exposures — preserving upside on their predictions.

The move represents an early attempt to build a financial layer on top of prediction-market infrastructure. As platforms like Polymarket have grown in volume — reaching hundreds of millions in monthly trading — auxiliary protocols targeting that ecosystem have begun to emerge, signaling broader DeFi integration with event-contract markets.

Why this matters

If Gondor gains traction, it could meaningfully increase capital efficiency for professional Polymarket traders, potentially driving higher position sizes and deeper liquidity in contested markets. Regulators watching event contracts may take note of DeFi credit products layered on prediction markets as a new compliance surface. For prediction-market operators, third-party lending protocols could become a competitive differentiator, pushing platforms to open APIs and composability features.
