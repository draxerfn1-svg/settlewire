Polymarket launches major U.S. comeback campaign after four-year ban

Published JUL 08, 2026 · 22:02 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Polymarket has launched an aggressive re-entry campaign targeting U.S. users in 2025, reversing the four-year exclusion that followed its 2022 CFTC settlement.

Polymarket, the world's largest prediction-market platform by volume, is mounting a deliberate push to recapture American users after being barred from the U.S. market following a $1.4 million CFTC settlement in January 2022, according to CoinDesk. The campaign marks the platform's most significant strategic move since that enforcement action forced it to block domestic participants.

The comeback effort comes at a moment of shifting regulatory winds in Washington, where the CFTC under the current administration has signaled a more permissive posture toward event contracts and prediction markets broadly. Rival domestic platforms such as Kalshi and ForecastEx have spent those four years building legal U.S. operations, giving Polymarket an established competitive field to re-enter.

Polymarket recorded billions of dollars in trading volume during the 2024 U.S. election cycle, driven largely by non-U.S. participants — underscoring the market appetite that a domestic re-launch could unlock. Whether the platform pursues a formal CFTC designation or another compliance path will be a key variable shaping the campaign's success, per CoinDesk's reporting.

Why this matters

A licensed Polymarket return to the U.S. would dramatically expand liquidity on political and macro event contracts, compressing spreads and potentially pressuring Kalshi's first-mover pricing advantage. Traders should watch for any CFTC designated contract market (DCM) application as a hard regulatory catalyst. If Polymarket operates without full licensing, it risks a second enforcement action that could chill the entire U.S. prediction-market sector.
