Better Markets Blasts CFTC Prediction Market Rules as Industry Capture

Published JUN 10, 2026 · 07:00 UTC · SettleWire newsroom

Advocacy group Better Markets has publicly condemned the CFTC's new prediction market framework, arguing the agency has abandoned its oversight mandate to serve industry interests.

Better Markets, a financial reform nonprofit, released a sharp critique of the CFTC's recently proposed prediction market rules, accusing the regulator of acting as a 'cheerleader' for the industry rather than a neutral watchdog. The group argues the rules tilt in favor of platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket at the expense of meaningful consumer protections.

The advocacy group contends that the CFTC's framework fails to adequately address risks associated with event contracts on politically sensitive topics, including elections and economic outcomes. Better Markets warns that lax oversight could open the door to manipulation and undermine public trust in both the markets and the regulatory process itself.

The critique lands as the CFTC finalizes rules that would govern how prediction market platforms operate under U.S. law. Kalshi's legal victories and Polymarket's surging volume have pressured regulators to produce a clearer framework, but critics say the resulting rules favor commercial operators over systemic safeguards.

Why this matters

For prediction-market traders and operators, a sustained advocacy campaign against CFTC rules could fuel congressional scrutiny or legal challenges that delay or revise the regulatory framework. If Better Markets' framing gains traction, platforms may face stricter contract-approval standards or leverage limits. Regulation watchers should monitor whether this pressure influences the final rulemaking timeline or triggers a formal comment-period extension.
